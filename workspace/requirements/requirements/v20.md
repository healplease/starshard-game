## V20.1 vision (one line)
A telegraphed area-denial enemy: a stationary ship **charges a harmless beam, then unleashes a
lethal, widening line that slowly sweeps across you and won't go away** — forcing you to read the
windup and move before it arms — built on a new **projectile-ownership** spine that finally lets the
game tell you **"Killed by &lt;name&gt;"**.

## V20.2 functional requirements (MoSCoW, testable)

Continues from R119 (R120+). "Shapes + text only / keyboard only / single screen" still applies
(C1, C2). v20 adds **one new enemy kind**, **a new timed multi-phase beam weapon**, a cross-cutting
**ownership field on ships + projectiles**, and **death-attribution tracking + one GAME_OVER copy
line**. All v20 requirements are **MUST**.

### A — The laser enemy (new enemy kind)

- **R120 — A new LASER enemy kind joins the roster.** The game adds a **new enemy `kind`** (alongside
  REGULAR / HEAVY / SCOUT) whose signature attack is the charged sweeping beam (R122–R126). It is an
  ordinary destroyable enemy ship in every other respect — it has HP, is killed by player fire and
  awards score on death exactly like the existing kinds, counts as an enemy at the destroy/award site
  (v14 R93), and is **visually distinguishable** from REGULAR/HEAVY/SCOUT and from both bosses (distinct
  shape/size/color — shapes+text only). Its **identity (internal kind key, on-screen name, shape,
  color, size), HP, score, and spawn behaviour are delegated** (Designer/Artist/Writer/Level-designer).
  It is **not** a boss (it spawns from the normal enemy stream, not the boss pool).

- **R121 — The laser enemy is IMMOBILE for the entire attack and repositions only between attacks.**
  While its beam attack is live — across **both** the windup and the damaging phase (R122) — the laser
  enemy **does not move** (it holds the firing position so the telegraph stays truthful). It may move
  to **reposition only during a cooldown gap between attacks** (the window when no beam of its own is
  live). The freeze MUST be driven by the ownership contract (R128): the enemy freezes itself because
  **its own** beam (matched by `source` = its ship ID) is active, not by a global flag — so two laser
  enemies firing at once each freeze independently. The reposition/cooldown movement pattern and
  durations are delegated (Designer/Level-designer).

### B — The charged sweeping beam (3-state weapon)

The beam is a **persistent, timed line segment** weapon (not a moving point bullet). It originates at
the firing enemy and extends to **slightly beyond a screen edge** so it reads as endless (R127). It
has three states (R122) with two behaviour phases — WINDUP then DAMAGING — and a between-attacks
COOLDOWN gap that belongs to the enemy, not the beam.

- **R122 — The beam runs WINDUP → DAMAGING, then is removed on TIMEOUT.** Each attack is a single
  beam instance with two sequential phases:
  - **WINDUP (~0.5 s)** — a **thin, HARMLESS telegraph line** from the enemy to just past the screen
    edge. The player **may pass through it with no damage** during windup. It signals where the lethal
    beam will arm.
  - **DAMAGING (~1 s)** — the beam becomes **lethal on contact** (R123), **widens over its lifetime**
    to a final thickness of **~5–7 px** (R124), and **sweeps toward the player at ~0.1× the player's
    move speed** (R125).
  - The beam **persists for its full DAMAGING duration and is removed only on TIMEOUT** (end of the
    damaging phase) — see R126. After timeout the enemy enters its cooldown gap and may reposition
    (R121). The two durations (windup, damaging) are delegated as concrete frame counts
    (Level-designer; defaults ~0.5 s / ~1 s).

- **R123 — Touching the DAMAGING beam damages the player; the WINDUP line never does.** A player whose
  damage hitbox (the v19 `P_HITBOX_R` circle) overlaps the beam **during the damaging phase** takes
  damage, subject to the existing invulnerability/i-frame rules (Shield + post-hit i-frames skip it,
  exactly like every other player-damage source — combat §V2.7 step 4). Contact during **windup deals
  zero damage**. The **per-hit damage amount and the damage cadence while touching** (per-frame tick vs
  once-per-contact) are delegated (Level-designer/Designer). The beam's collision is a **line/segment
  test against the player hitbox at the beam's current width**, not a circle.

- **R124 — The DAMAGING beam WIDENS over its lifetime to ~5–7 px.** During the damaging phase the
  beam's thickness grows from a thin line toward a final width of **~5–7 px** over the phase duration.
  The beam's **collision width tracks its drawn width** (draw == collision, no invisible reach), so a
  player grazing the early-thin beam may be missed where the late-wide beam would hit. The growth curve
  (linear vs eased), the start width, and the exact final width are delegated (Level-designer/Artist).

- **R125 — The DAMAGING beam SWEEPS toward the player at ~0.1× player move speed.** During the damaging
  phase the beam moves toward the player at **~0.1× the player's move speed** (`P_SPEED`), slowly
  enough that an alert player can out-walk it. "Sweeps toward the player" means the beam's anchor/angle
  drifts in the player's direction at fire-relevant time; **whether the sweep tracks the player live or
  aims at a fire-time-frozen target, and whether the beam rotates about the enemy or translates, are
  delegated** (Designer/Level-designer). The sweep speed magnitude (default ~0.1× `P_SPEED`) is the
  Level-designer's.

- **R126 — The beam is removed ONLY on TIMEOUT — never on contact.** Hitting the player does **not**
  consume or end the beam (unlike a normal bullet, which is consumed on the hit). The beam stays live —
  damaging, widening, and sweeping — for the **full** damaging duration regardless of how many times it
  has touched the player, and is removed **only** when its damaging phase times out. (This is the crux
  that distinguishes the beam from every existing projectile; AC checks the beam survives a contact.)

- **R127 — The beam is a line from the enemy to just past a screen edge (endless look).** The beam
  geometry is a **line segment originating at the firing enemy** and extending to a point **slightly
  beyond a screen edge**, so it appears to run off-screen endlessly. The exact origin anchor on the
  ship, which edge it extends past, the overshoot amount, and whether it points straight or toward the
  player are delegated (Designer/Artist/Level-designer); the contract is only that it is an
  enemy-anchored line reaching past the visible play area.

### C — Projectile ownership (ship IDs + projectile `source`) — cross-cutting

- **R128 — Every ship has a unique ID; every projectile carries a `source` = the firing ship's ID.**
  Every ship in the world — **the player, every enemy (all kinds incl. the laser enemy), and every
  boss** — is assigned a **unique identifier**. Every projectile the game creates — **player bullets,
  enemy bullets, GREEN/RED/CYAN/YELLOW/NOVA bullets, the laser beam**, i.e. **player shots included for
  consistency** — carries a **`source` field set to the ID of the ship that fired it**. The
  representation (incrementing counter, enum+id, object ref, etc.) is the **Programmer's** call; the
  contract is that **every ship is uniquely identifiable** and **every projectile is attributable to
  exactly one firing ship** via `source`. IDs need not persist across runs (a restart may re-issue
  them); they must be unique **within a run**. Adding `source`/IDs MUST NOT change any existing
  projectile motion, spawn, collision, or culling behaviour (no AC1–AC108 regression).

- **R129 — The laser owner freezes itself while its own beam is live, via `source`.** The laser enemy
  uses the ownership contract to implement R121: it is frozen **iff a live beam exists whose `source`
  equals this enemy's ID**. This MUST be `source`-driven (each enemy keyed to its own beam), so the
  freeze is correct when multiple laser enemies coexist and is automatically lifted when the owner's
  beam times out.

### D — Death attribution → "Killed by &lt;name&gt;" on GAME_OVER

- **R130 — The game tracks what dealt the killing blow.** When the player's HP reaches 0, the game
  records the **lethal source** of the final damage:
  - **Enemy/asteroid/boss BODY collision** → that body's entity (asteroid/debris, the rammed enemy of
    its kind, or the boss).
  - **Projectile collision** (any enemy/boss bullet **or the laser beam**) → the **owning ship**,
    resolved via the projectile's `source` (R128) back to that ship's identity.
  The lethal source is the source of the **damage instance that drove HP ≤ 0** (the "first hit wins"
  damage already resolved in combat §V2.7 step 4). This adds tracking only; it MUST NOT change which
  hit lands or any damage value (no combat regression).

- **R131 — GAME_OVER shows "Killed by &lt;enemy name&gt;"; every lethal source has a display name,
  with a fallback for unknown.** The GAME_OVER screen displays a line of the form
  **"Killed by &lt;name&gt;"** naming the recorded lethal source (R130). **Every** source that can
  deal the killing blow MUST map to a **display name** — including all existing ones:
  **asteroid/debris, REGULAR, HEAVY, SCOUT, both bosses (Mothership + NOVA), and the laser enemy** —
  resolved for a projectile kill via the owning ship's name (R130). If the lethal source cannot be
  determined (e.g. it was destroyed/swept before attribution, or a future source has no name), the
  line uses a **defined fallback name** rather than crashing or showing a blank/raw ID. The **exact
  wording/format of the line and the display name for every source (including the fallback string)**
  are the **Writer's** (downstream); this requirement locks only the contract (which sources, the
  projectile→owner-name mapping, and that a fallback exists).

### E — Smoke + tests (MUST)

- **R132 — Smoke gate green, full laser cycle + attribution exercised, suite grows, no regression.**
  After v20, `main.py --smoke-test` still **exits 0 after exactly 120 frames** headless (R14
  preserved) with **no AC1–AC108 regression**. The smoke/test path **MUST exercise a full laser cycle**
  (WINDUP → DAMAGING, including the beam widening and sweeping and surviving a player contact) — seeded

  source resolves to the right name, for at least one enemy-body and one projectile/beam case). The
  **pytest suite stays green and grows** with checks for: harmless windup vs lethal damaging (R122/R123),
  beam widening (R124) and sweep ~0.1× (R125), beam persists-through-contact-to-timeout (R126),
  firer-immobile-while-firing + reposition-between (R121/R129), `source`/ID assignment on every
  projectile incl. player shots (R128), and attribution mapping each lethal source → its name with the
  unknown fallback (R130/R131) — while AC1–AC108 still hold.

## V20.5 non-goals (still out of scope)
- ❌ Changing existing **enemy/boss movesets, stats, HP, spawn behaviour**, or the **bonus economy /
  HP / score** (only the added `source`/ID field touches projectiles, transparently).
- ❌ Changing **screen flow** — only GAME_OVER gains the "Killed by &lt;name&gt;" line; START / PLAY /
  PAUSE / STATS are unchanged.
- ❌ Changing **player controls**, the v19 hitbox/bullet sizing, or any existing copy except the new
  death line + the laser-enemy name.
- ❌ Making the laser a **boss** or adding it to `BOSS_POOL` — it spawns from the normal enemy stream.
- ❌ A beam that **disappears on contact**, a beam that damages during **windup**, or a firer that
  **moves while its beam is live** — all explicitly forbidden (R121/R123/R126).
- ❌ Persisting ship IDs across runs, surfacing IDs in the UI, or any new screen / persistence /
  external assets/audio (C1/C2).

## V20.6 risks / watch-items
- **Beam-consumed-on-hit reflex.** The whole collision pipeline consumes a bullet on the hit; the beam
  must be exempted (R126). → AC114 checks the beam survives a contact and runs to timeout.
- **Windup armed too early.** A telegraph that damages during windup defeats the "read-and-dodge"
  design. → R123 forbids it; AC111 checks 0 damage during windup.
- **Invisible reach.** A beam whose collision width exceeds its drawn width is unfair. → R124 ties
  collision width to drawn width; AC112 checks it.
- **Unavoidable beam.** A sweep that's too fast, or a beam that arms on top of the player with no room,
  can be undodgeable. → R125 caps the sweep at ~0.1× `P_SPEED`; Level-designer confirms no unavoidable
  beam (fairness is a level-design gate); AC113 checks out-walkability.
- **Ownership field regresses motion.** Adding `source`/IDs naively could perturb spawn or collision. →
  R128 requires it be additive only; AC116/AC119 check no projectile-behaviour change.
- **Attribution races the sweep.** If the lethal source can be culled before GAME_OVER reads it (the
  beam persists, but a rammed enemy may be consumed), attribution must capture the **name at damage
  time**, not chase a possibly-dead entity. → R130 records the source at the lethal-damage instant; R131
  guarantees a fallback so a missing source never blanks/crashes the line.
- **A lethal source with no name.** Every current source must be named, not just the new enemy. → R131
  enumerates all of them (incl. legacy) + a fallback; AC118 checks each.
- **Run-length.** A stationary, telegraphed enemy is dodgeable and could extend runs. → spawn weight /
  earliest-spawn are Level-designer levers; keep AC13 (1–3 min).
