# v6 increment — Bombs (panic button) + control remap (Z fire / X bomb)

Owner: business-analyst · Date: 2026-06-05 · Status: complete
Source: `workspace/shared/brief.md` (v6 increment section), `CLAUDE.md` (scope guardrails),
`workspace/shared/backlog.md` v6 row 2

> This section **appends** to v1 + v2 + v5; it does not replace them. All prior requirements
> (R1–R44) and acceptance criteria (AC1–AC29) still hold. v6 adds a **player-triggered bomb** (a
> screen-flushing panic button) on a **small, replenishable charge pool**, its **activation flash**, a
> **new bomb-charge bonus pickup** that reuses the v2 pickup framework (R23/R28/R30/R31), and a
> **control remap** (fire → **Z**, bomb → **X**). It continues numbering from R44 / AC29.
> This section fixes *behaviour and rules*; every concrete number is delegated — see the **Open values**
> table in §20. The bomb is the long-deferred v2 R35 "screen-clear / bomb" idea, now promoted, charge-
> gated, and fully specified.

## 18. v6 vision (one line)
When the screen gets overwhelming you slam **X**: a flash of light **flushes everything hostile off the
screen** — but you only carry **2 charges**, refilled only by a **rare pickup**, so a bomb is a hard
choice, not a crutch.

## 19. v6 functional requirements (MoSCoW, testable)

Continues numbering from v5 (R45+). "Shapes + text only / keyboard only / single screen" still applies
(C1, C2). Concrete numbers (charge cap, flush score rule, pickup scarcity, flash timing, inter-bomb
lockout) are **delegated** to the design roles — see §20. The examples below are illustrative only.

### MUST have (v6 — the increment is not complete without these)

- **R45 — Bomb charge pool.** The player has an integer **bomb-charge count**, starting each run at
  **exactly 2**. It is bounded by a **max cap** (a designer value, **≥ 2**, §20). The count is
  player-visible on the HUD at all times during play (readout style = Artist + Writer). The count only
  ever **decreases by activation (R46)** and **increases by the bomb pickup (R51)**, each by exactly 1;
  it is **clamped to [0, cap]** and is never negative and never above the cap. **Restart (R13) resets
  the count to the starting 2** (no carry-over between runs — extends R31's restart-cleanup rule).

- **R46 — Bomb activation (X).** Pressing the **bomb key (X)** while the **charge count is > 0** during
  active play **consumes exactly one charge** and triggers, on that activation, **both** the screen
  flush (R48) **and** the activation flash (R50). Activation is **edge-triggered on key-down** (a single
  press = a single bomb; see R53), and is available only during normal play (not on the start screen,
  game-over screen, or while paused if pause exists).

- **R47 — Out-of-bombs guard.** While the **charge count is 0**, pressing **X is a complete no-op**:
  **no flush, no flash, no decrement, no state change** — the count stays at 0 and play continues
  unchanged. (This is the explicit failure case of R46.)

- **R48 — Screen-flush effect (what the bomb destroys).** On activation the bomb **instantaneously
  clears all currently on-screen hostile entities**, namely:
  - **all enemies** of every kind (REGULAR / HEAVY / SCOUT) — removed from play;
  - **all asteroids / debris hazards** — removed from play;
  - **all enemy projectiles**, explicitly **including in-flight HEAVY green splitting pellets and any
    already-split RED child bullets** (and SCOUT cyan, REGULAR red) — removed from play.
  Removal uses the **same entity lists / despawn machinery** that already exist (R8/R9 entities); it is a
  bulk clear of the live lists at the activation instant.
  - **BA rulings (NOT delegated) on the other on-screen objects:**
    - **Player bullets — UNAFFECTED.** Player shots already in flight are **not** removed (they are
      harmless to the player and clearing them has no gameplay value); they continue and despawn
      normally.
    - **Pickups (all v2 bonuses + the new bomb pickup, R51) — UNAFFECTED / SURVIVE the flush.** A bomb
      must **never** destroy collectible pickups (flushing a bomb pickup would be self-defeating); they
      keep drifting and remain collectible.
    - **Particles / hit-feedback / starfield — UNAFFECTED at the gameplay level.** These are cosmetic,
      not damaging entities; the flush does not need to touch them (the Artist may add a bomb-specific
      burst as an option, not a requirement).
  - **Does a bomb-kill award score? — DELEGATED (flush score rule, §20).** Whether destroying entities
    via the flush grants score (**none / partial / flat-per-kill**) is a **balance value owned by the
    Designer**. Whatever rule is chosen must be **deterministic** and must not corrupt the R11 score
    display. (Default recommendation, non-binding: **no score** for bomb-kills, so the bomb is a survival
    tool rather than a farming exploit — Designer confirms.)

- **R49 — Flush scope is "currently on-screen"; spawning resumes normally.** The flush acts on the
  entities **alive at the activation instant**. It does **not** permanently suppress spawning: the normal
  spawner (v1 ramp + v5 kind-mix + v2 bonus economy) **resumes immediately** on subsequent frames, so
  the screen can repopulate. Any **brief post-bomb spawn lull / breather** is **optional and a designer
  value** (§20, COULD-grade); absent a specified lull, spawning simply continues as before. (This
  resolves the brief's "whether it also cancels in-flight spawns" question: the bomb clears what is on
  screen now; it is **not** a timed spawn-suppression field.)

- **R50 — Activation flash (full-screen).** Each bomb activation plays a **brief full-screen flash**: a
  **screen-covering bright overlay that appears and fades out** over a short duration. It is **purely
  visual** — it does **not** block input or pause the simulation beyond its own frames, and it renders as
  a **shape/overlay only** (no external asset, C2). The flash **color, opacity, and duration** are
  **Artist (+ Designer timing) values** (§20). The flash is tied 1:1 to a *successful* activation (R46),
  so a 0-charge no-op (R47) produces **no flash**.

- **R51 — Bomb-charge bonus pickup (the only replenishment).** A **new bonus pickup kind — the bomb
  pickup — is added to the v2 pickup framework (R23)**: it spawns and **drifts downward with the scroll**,
  is drawn as a **shape + identifying glyph/letter** (Artist + Writer), and is **collected by flying over
  it** using the same R23 collision machinery. **Collecting it restores exactly +1 bomb charge**,
  **clamped to the cap (R45)** — it can **never** push the count above the cap. **Collecting at full cap**
  is either **wasted (consumed, no effect)** or **refused (left in play)** — that choice is a **designer
  value** (§20); either way the count must not exceed the cap. The bomb pickup is the **sole** way to
  replenish charges (no time-based regen, no enemy-kill regen unless the Level-designer routes it through
  this pickup). It is an **instant, one-shot** effect (like Repair R24): **no timer, no HUD buff-pill**
  (it only changes the R45 charge readout) — so it is exempt from the R29 active-buff-timer treatment.

- **R52 — Control remap (Z = fire, X = bomb).** The keymap is remapped:
  - **Z fires the weapon** — Z replaces the v1 fire key for **R7/R8** firing, and **still respects the
    existing fire-rate cooldown** (incl. v2 Rapid/Fan modifiers R25/R26). Whether the old fire key
    (Space) is **retained as a secondary alias** is the Programmer's discretion, but **Z is the canonical
    fire key** and all on-screen copy must teach **Z**.
  - **X activates the bomb** (R46).
  - **Movement keys (arrows / WASD per R3) and the start / restart / quit keys (R13/R16) are
    unchanged.**
  - The **in-game controls text and the start-screen instructions copy MUST be updated** to show the new
    binding (**Z = fire, X = bomb**) so controls stay discoverable (R16). Final wording = **Writer**.

- **R53 — One press = one bomb (input safety).** A bomb activation is **edge-triggered**: holding X down
  must **not** drain multiple charges across consecutive frames, and a single physical press must consume
  **at most one** charge and fire **at most one** flush+flash. An optional **inter-bomb cooldown / input
  lockout** (a few frames) to prevent same-press double-consume is a **designer value** (§20); even
  without it, the key-down edge gate alone must guarantee one-press-one-bomb.

- **R54 — Smoke gate preserved + bomb activation seeded.** After v6, `workspace/game/main.py
  --smoke-test` still **initializes pygame, runs exactly 120 frames with simulated input, and exits 0**
  under `SDL_VIDEODRIVER=dummy` / `SDL_AUDIODRIVER=dummy` (no window, no audio, no hang) — R14/R33/R43
  preserved. Additionally, the smoke run **must exercise at least one full bomb activation lifecycle
  headlessly** (charge > 0 → X activates → flush clears the live hostile lists → flash overlay appears
  and fades → charge decrements) **without raising**. Because 120 frames may not naturally leave the
  player with a charge and a press at the right moment, the smoke path **must seed a bomb activation**
  (mechanism — simulated X press on a known frame, forced activation hook, or pre-seeded charge + input —
  is the Programmer's choice; the **requirement is that the flush + flash actually run in `--smoke-test`**).

### COULD have (only if cheap and in-scope — designer's discretion)

- **R55 — Post-bomb breather.** A short, specified **spawn lull** immediately after a bomb (a few hundred
  ms of reduced/paused spawning) to make the flush feel impactful. Optional (R49); Level-designer's call,
  must not break AC13.

> Extras (R55) are acceptance-checked **only if implemented**; their absence does not fail v6 as long as
> R45–R54 pass.

## 21. v6 non-goals (still out of scope)
- ❌ Multiple bomb *types* or a selectable bomb inventory — there is exactly **one** bomb effect (the
  flush) on **one** key (X).
- ❌ A held/charged bomb, a partial-screen or directional blast, or a bomb that damages-over-time — the
  flush is an **instantaneous full-screen clear** (R48).
- ❌ Time-based or kill-based charge regeneration — charges refill **only** via the bomb pickup (R51).
- ❌ Carrying bomb charges **across runs** or saving them to disk — restart fully resets (R45/R31).
- ❌ Bomb-triggered i-frames, slow-time, or any effect beyond clearing hostiles + the visual flash.
- ❌ External flash/sound assets — the flash is a **shape/overlay** only (C2); no audio (C2).
- ❌ Mouse / gamepad bomb activation — keyboard-only (C1); bomb is **X**, fire is **Z**.

## 22. v6 risks / watch-items
- **Double-consume on one press** is the top input risk: if X is read as level-held instead of
  edge-triggered, a single press drains both charges in two frames → R53 mandates a key-down edge gate;
  AC36 checks one-press-one-bomb.
- **Flushing the wrong list:** the clear must hit **enemy** projectiles incl. green pellets + red split
  children but **spare pickups and player bullets** (R48 rulings) — a too-broad clear that eats pickups
  makes the bomb self-defeating; a too-narrow clear that misses split children leaves the player hit →
  flag to Programmer; AC32 enumerates the lists.
- **Charge over/underflow:** collecting at full cap, or activating at 0, must clamp — never exceed cap,
  never go negative (R45/R47/R51) → AC31/AC34 check the bounds.
- **Remap regression:** moving fire to Z must not break the R7 cooldown or v2 Rapid/Fan modifiers, and
  the **start-screen copy must actually change** (a stale "press Space" string is a defect) → AC35.
- **Smoke coverage gap:** a natural 120-frame run may never leave the player a charge + a press at the
  right time → the flush/flash would go unverified → R54 requires the smoke path to **seed** an
  activation → AC37.
- **Activation-feel edge cases (for QA):** is the bomb usable while in R18/R27 i-frames, or on the same
  frame the player takes damage? These don't change the charge math but the Designer should state the
  intent (default: bomb is always usable when charges > 0, independent of i-frame state) → noted for the
  Designer + QA.

## 23. v6 acceptance criteria (what QA will check)

Continues AC numbering (AC30+). **[Smoke]** = headless via `--smoke-test`; **[Play]** = by observation;
**[Code]** = by inspection. The brief's headline AC — *bomb activates on X only when charges > 0; clears
the specified entities; plays the flash; decrements the pool; 0-charge press is a no-op; the pickup
restores exactly one charge respecting the cap; Z fires / X bombs; smoke still exits 0 with a bomb
activation exercised* — is covered by AC30–AC37.

- **AC30 [Play/Smoke]** Pressing **X with charges > 0** during play **consumes exactly one charge** and
  triggers **both** the flush (AC32) and the flash (AC33); the HUD charge readout **decrements by 1**
  (R45, R46, R50).
- **AC31 [Play/Smoke]** With the charge count at **0**, pressing **X is a no-op** — **no flush, no flash,
  no decrement**, count stays 0, play continues unchanged (R47).
- **AC32 [Play/Smoke]** A bomb activation **removes all on-screen enemies (REGULAR/HEAVY/SCOUT), all
  asteroids/debris, and all enemy bullets — including in-flight green pellets and already-split red
  children**; **pickups (v2 bonuses + bomb pickup) and in-flight player bullets are NOT removed** (R48).
- **AC33 [Play]** A successful activation shows a **brief full-screen flash** (a bright screen-covering
  overlay that **fades out**) and then play continues; a 0-charge no-op produces **no flash** (R50, R47).
- **AC34 [Play/Smoke]** Collecting a **bomb pickup** raises the charge count by **exactly 1**, **clamped
  to the cap** (never exceeds it); collecting at full cap obeys the chosen waste/refuse rule and still
  does not exceed the cap; the pickup is **scarcer** than the power-ups (R51).
- **AC35 [Play]** After the remap, **Z fires** the weapon (respecting the R7 cooldown and v2 Rapid/Fan
  modifiers) and **X bombs**; **movement / restart / quit keys are unchanged**; the **in-game controls
  and start-screen copy show "Z = fire, X = bomb"** (no stale fire-key text) (R52).
- **AC36 [Play/Smoke]** A **single X press consumes at most one charge and fires at most one** flush+flash
  (holding X does not drain multiple charges); after **restart (R13)** the charge count **resets to 2**
  and no flash/bomb state leaks into the new run (R53, R45/R31).
- **AC37 [Smoke]** `main.py --smoke-test` **exits 0 after exactly 120 frames** headless (R14/R33/R43
  preserved) **and** the smoke run **exercises at least one full bomb activation lifecycle** (charge → X
  activates → flush clears live hostile lists → flash appears/fades → charge decrements) **without
  raising** (R54).
- **AC38 [Code]** The bomb reuses **existing entity lists** for the flush and the **v2 pickup path** for
  the bomb pickup; the charge count is an explicit clamped integer state; the flash and HUD readout are
  **shapes/overlays only**; **no external assets** are added (R45, R48, R51; C2).

> v6 is complete when **R45–R54 (MUST)** are met and **AC30–AC38** pass. All v1 MUST items (R1–R14), v2
> MUST items (R23–R33), and v5 MUST items (R36–R44), plus their smoke gates, must **still** pass (no
> regression). R55 is bonus-only.
