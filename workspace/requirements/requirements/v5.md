# v5 increment — Three enemy types (varied movement, fire & bullet behaviors)

Owner: business-analyst · Date: 2026-06-05 · Status: complete
Source: `workspace/shared/brief.md` (v5 increment section), `CLAUDE.md` (scope guardrails),
`workspace/shared/backlog.md` v5 row 2

> This section **appends** to v1 + v2; it does not replace them. All prior requirements
> (R1–R35) and acceptance criteria (AC1–AC21) still hold. v5 **generalizes R6** ("an enemy that
> shoots back") into a **typed enemy roster** (R36–R41) and continues numbering from R35 / AC21.
> Today the game has exactly one enemy that fires a single red bullet aimed dead-on at the player's
> current position (`entities/hazards.py` `Enemy`, `entities/projectiles.py` `make_enemy_bullet`,
> `EB_SPEED`). v5 keeps that enemy **with one tweak** (aim deviation) and adds **two new kinds**.

## 13. v5 vision (one line)
The cosmos gets more dangerous: alongside the familiar fighter (now firing a touch *off* your exact
position), a slow **Heavy** lobs a green pellet that **bursts into a 3-bullet red fan** partway to you,
and a quick **Scout** snipes with fast, accurate **cyan** fire — three readable threats, each demanding
a different dodge.

## 14. v5 functional requirements (MoSCoW, testable)

Continues numbering from v2 (R36+). "Shapes + text only / keyboard only / single screen" still applies
(C1, C2). **This section fixes *behaviour and rules*; every concrete number is delegated** — see the
**Open values** table in §15. The design roles (lead-game-designer GDD, level-designer level_spec) own
the numbers; do not read final tuning into the examples below (they are illustrative only).

### MUST have (v5 — the increment is not complete without these)

- **R36 — Typed enemy roster.** The game supports **three distinct enemy types** — **REGULAR**,
  **HEAVY**, **SCOUT** — each with its own **body size**, **move speed/behavior**, **bullet color**,
  **bullet velocity**, **fire cadence**, and **aim accuracy** (deviation cone). The type is a property
  of the enemy (an internal **kind** identifier — Writer names it, R44), so spawning, rendering, and
  firing can branch on it. All three are **enemies that shoot back** in the sense of R6/AC6 (their
  bullets damage the player on contact per R9), and all remain destroyable by player fire (R8) — HP per
  type is a designer value (Heavy may take more hits; Scout may be fragile).

- **R37 — Regular enemy + aim deviation (existing kind, one tweak).** The REGULAR enemy is the
  **current** fighter: same descend-then-strafe movement, firing **RED** bullets at **MEDIUM** velocity.
  **Change:** its shots are aimed at the player's **general area**, not dead-on — each shot's heading is
  the direction to the player **perturbed by a random angle within a moderate deviation cone** (the
  *regular* cone half-angle is a designer value, §15). The deviation is **per-shot random** within the
  cone (not a fixed lead/offset). With the cone set to zero the behavior must reduce to today's exact
  aim — i.e. deviation is an additive perturbation on the existing aim vector, so R6/AC6 still hold.

- **R38 — Heavy enemy (new kind).** The HEAVY enemy is **larger** and **moves slower** than the
  REGULAR (both deltas are designer values). It fires a **GREEN** pellet at **MEDIUM** velocity toward
  the player. The green pellet is a **splitting projectile** (its full lifecycle is specified in R39).
  Heavy's fire cadence, size, move speed, and HP are designer values; it is otherwise an ordinary enemy
  for spawning, collision, scoring, and the existing enemy-drop roll (it may drop a v2 bonus on death
  like any enemy, unless the designer says otherwise).

- **R39 — Splitting green pellet (the Heavy's projectile).** The green pellet's lifecycle:
  1. **Travel.** On spawn it records its **heading** (the unit direction it was fired, i.e. toward the
     player's position **at fire time**) and travels in a straight line at its medium velocity. It is a
     normal damaging enemy projectile during this phase (contact damages the player per R9).
  2. **Split trigger ("midway").** When it reaches the **split point** it **disappears** (is removed)
     and is replaced by its children. "Midway" is defined as a **fraction of the original fire-time
     pellet→player distance** (e.g. ~0.5; the **split fraction** is a designer value, §15). Because the
     player may have moved, the trigger must use a **frozen reference** captured at fire time — the
     split point is a **fixed distance/position along the pellet's own heading** (or an equivalent
     **timer** derived from that distance ÷ pellet speed), **not** a live re-read of the player's
     current position. The pellet must **always split** if it survives to the split point (it does not
     re-home).
  3. **Children.** The split produces **exactly 3 RED bullets** that **preserve the green pellet's
     heading** and **spread in a symmetric fan** about that heading — typically one along the heading
     and one on each side at ±(fan angle) (the **fan half-angle** and whether the center child is
     included are designer values, §15; the result is 3 bullets fanned around the original heading).
  4. **Children are ordinary red bullets.** After the split, each child behaves **exactly like an
     ordinary enemy red bullet** (R6/R9 damage, same despawn-off-screen rules); they do **not** split
     again. Child velocity (same as the green pellet's, or a designer-chosen red-bullet speed) is a
     designer value.
  - **Edge cases:** if the pellet is destroyed or leaves the screen **before** the split point, **no
    children are produced** (only a surviving pellet splits). The split itself is **unconditional on the
    player's position** — even if the player is gone/dead, a surviving pellet still splits into the fan.

- **R40 — Scout enemy (new kind).** The SCOUT enemy **moves faster** than the REGULAR (delta is a
  designer value). It fires **CYAN** bullets at **HIGH** velocity toward the player with **high
  accuracy** — a **tight or zero deviation cone** (the *scout* cone half-angle is a designer value and
  is **smaller than the regular cone**, §15; zero = perfect aim is acceptable). Scout cyan bullets are
  ordinary single damaging enemy projectiles (no split). Scout size, fire cadence, and HP are designer
  values; a faster/accurate sniper is typically **fragile** (low HP) but that balance is the designer's
  call.

- **R41 — Spawn mix, weighting & difficulty integration.** The spawner selects which enemy **kind** to
  spawn using a **weighting / mix** that **folds into the existing level ramp** (`levels/level_spec.md`,
  R15). The per-type spawn weights (and any time-gating, e.g. Scouts appearing only after some ramp
  threshold) are **level-designer values**. The mix **must not break AC13** (runs still typically end
  within ~1–3 minutes) and must not regress the v1/v2 ramp. At least the REGULAR kind spawns from the
  start so R6/AC6 continue to hold without the new kinds.

- **R42 — Visual distinguishability (shapes + colors).** The three enemy bodies are **visually
  distinguishable at a glance** as **placeholder shapes** (distinct shape and/or size per kind), and the
  three bullet families are distinguishable by **color**: **RED** (regular + split children), **GREEN**
  (heavy pellet), **CYAN** (scout). Exact shapes, sizes, and hex values are the **Artist's** call
  (`art/art_spec.md`), coordinating with the existing palette; **no external image/sound assets** (C2).
  The split should read on screen as "green pellet → three red bullets" (a brief split burst/feedback is
  the Artist's option, not required).

- **R43 — Smoke gate preserved + split lifecycle seeded.** After v5, `workspace/game/main.py
  --smoke-test` still **initializes pygame, runs exactly 120 frames with simulated input, and exits 0**
  under `SDL_VIDEODRIVER=dummy` / `SDL_AUDIODRIVER=dummy` (no window, no audio, no hang) — R14/R33
  preserved. Additionally, the smoke run **must exercise at least one full green-pellet split lifecycle
  headlessly** (a Heavy fires → its pellet reaches the split point → it is replaced by the 3-red fan →
  the children update as ordinary bullets) **without raising**. Because 120 frames may not naturally
  spawn a Heavy and let its pellet reach the split point, the smoke path **must seed at least one
  Heavy + green pellet so the split executes** within the frame budget (mechanism — forced spawn,
  pre-seeded pellet near its split point, or smoke-only hook — is the Programmer's choice; the
  **requirement is that the split actually runs in `--smoke-test`**).

- **R44 — Internal kind names / any UI labels.** Each enemy type has a stable **internal kind name**
  (used in code/specs/QA: e.g. `REGULAR` / `HEAVY` / `SCOUT`, final wording the **Writer's** call). If
  any enemy type is **surfaced in the UI** (it likely is **not** in this scope — no bestiary/HUD enemy
  labels are required), the on-screen wording is the Writer's. Absent a UI surface, R44 is satisfied by
  the Writer **blessing the internal kind names** for consistent use across specs and code.

### Out of scope for v5 (non-goals)
- ❌ More than these three enemy types, enemy elites/bosses, or per-type weapons beyond the one
  bullet-behavior each (regular aimed-red, heavy splitting-green, scout fast-cyan).
- ❌ Recursive / multi-generation splitting — green children are **terminal** ordinary red bullets
  (R39.4); they never split again.
- ❌ Homing/re-targeting projectiles — the green pellet commits to its fire-time heading (R39.2); no
  bullet tracks the player after firing.
- ❌ New external assets, sounds, input modes, or extra screens (C1, C2 still hold).
- ❌ Per-enemy health bars or a bestiary UI (R44 stays internal unless the Writer/Artist add minimal
  flavor within scope).

## 15. Open values (delegated — design roles fix these; do NOT treat examples as final)

The BA names the levers; the **Designer/Level-designer set the numbers**. None below are requirements
on a specific value — only that the value **exists and is specified** by the owning role.

| Lever | Applies to | Owner | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Body **size** per kind | regular / heavy / scout | Designer (+ Artist for shape) | Heavy **>** regular; scout ≈ or **<** regular (small) |
| **Move speed** per kind | regular / heavy / scout | Designer | Heavy **slower**, Scout **faster** than regular |
| **HP** per kind | regular / heavy / scout | Designer | Heavy likely tankier; Scout likely fragile |
| **Bullet speed** | red (medium) / green (medium) / cyan (high) | Designer | Cyan **>** red ≈ green; "medium"/"high" relative to today's `EB_SPEED` |
| **Fire cadence** per kind | regular / heavy / scout | Designer | interval between shots per type |
| **Aim deviation cone** (half-angle) | regular (moderate), scout (tight/zero) | Designer | scout cone **<** regular cone; 0 = perfect aim allowed |
| **Split fraction** ("midway") | green pellet | Designer | fraction of fire-time pellet→player distance, e.g. ~0.5 |
| **Fan half-angle** + center-child rule | green split → 3 red children | Designer | symmetric spread about heading; result = 3 bullets |
| **Child bullet speed** | green split children | Designer | same as pellet, or a chosen red-bullet speed |
| **Spawn weighting / mix** + time-gating | all three kinds | Level-designer | folds into the ramp; must keep AC13 |

## 16. v5 risks / watch-items
- **"Midway" ambiguity** is the top risk: if the split re-reads the player's *current* position it will
  feel like homing and can split at distance ≈0 when the player closed in. R39.2 mandates a
  **fire-time-frozen** reference (distance/heading captured at fire) → flag to Programmer; AC25 checks it.
- **Smoke coverage gap:** a Heavy may not spawn (or its pellet may not reach the split point) within 120
  frames → the split goes unverified. R43 requires the smoke path to **seed** the split → AC27.
- **Difficulty creep:** three firing patterns at once (esp. Scout's fast accurate cyan) can spike
  lethality and push runs under the 1–3 min band → level-designer tunes weights to protect AC13 (R41).
- **Readability:** three enemy bodies + three bullet colors must stay distinguishable as plain shapes →
  Artist owns distinct shapes/sizes (R42); avoid colors clashing with existing hazards/bonuses/HUD.
- **Regression:** generalizing the single enemy into a roster must not break existing R6/AC6 (regular
  still shoots), v1/v2 collision/scoring, or the bonus drop roll (R38) → keep REGULAR's zero-cone
  behavior identical to today.

## 17. v5 acceptance criteria (what QA will check)

Continues AC numbering (AC22+). **[Smoke]** = headless via `--smoke-test`; **[Play]** = by observation;
**[Code]** = by inspection. The brief's headline AC — *all three enemies spawn & move per spec; regular
fires with deviation; heavy's green pellet splits midway into a 3-red fan preserving heading; scout
fires fast/accurate cyan; smoke still exits 0 with a split exercised* — is covered by AC22–AC27.

- **AC22 [Play]** All **three enemy kinds** appear in play and are **visually distinguishable** (distinct
  body shape/size) — REGULAR, HEAVY (bigger/slower), SCOUT (faster) — each moving per its spec (R36,
  R38, R40, R42).
- **AC23 [Play]** The **REGULAR** enemy fires **red** medium-velocity bullets aimed at the player's
  **general area** (visible spread shot-to-shot, not dead-on), and these bullets still damage the player
  (R37, R9). With the regular cone configured to zero, aim reduces to exact tracking (R37).
- **AC24 [Play]** The **HEAVY** enemy fires a **green** medium-velocity pellet toward the player (R38).
- **AC25 [Play/Smoke]** The green pellet **splits at the midway point** into **exactly 3 red bullets**
  that **preserve the pellet's heading** and **spread in a fan**; after splitting the children behave as
  **ordinary red enemy bullets** (damage on contact, no further splitting). The split point is based on
  the **fire-time** pellet→player distance, **not** the player's live position (R39).
- **AC26 [Play]** The **SCOUT** enemy fires **cyan high-velocity** bullets with **high accuracy**
  (little/no deviation — visibly tighter/faster than the regular's red fire) that damage the player
  (R40, R9).
- **AC27 [Smoke]** `main.py --smoke-test` **exits 0 after exactly 120 frames** headless (R14/R33
  preserved) **and** the smoke run **exercises at least one full green-pellet split lifecycle** (pellet →
  3-red fan → children update) **without raising** (R43).
- **AC28 [Play]** The **spawn mix** produces all three kinds over a run, folded into the difficulty ramp,
  and a run still **typically ends within ~1–3 minutes** (AC13 not regressed) (R41).
- **AC29 [Code]** Enemy **kind** is an explicit property branched on for spawn/render/fire; REGULAR's
  behavior with zero deviation matches the pre-v5 enemy (no regression to R6/AC6); green children reuse
  the ordinary enemy-bullet path; no external assets added (R36, R37, R42; C2).

> v5 is complete when **R36–R44 (MUST)** are met and **AC22–AC29** pass. All v1 MUST items (R1–R14) and
> v2 MUST items (R23–R33), plus their smoke gates, must **still** pass (no regression).
