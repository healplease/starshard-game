# v12 increment — Hold-R-to-restart (mirror the Q-hold gesture on PAUSE + GAME_OVER)

Owner: business-analyst · Date: 2026-06-05 · Status: in progress
Source: `workspace/shared/brief.md` (v12 increment, via backlog), `workspace/shared/backlog.md` v12
row 2, and `CLAUDE.md` (scope guardrails).

> This section **appends** to v1 + v2 + v5 + v6 + v7 + v8 + v10 (v11 was an art-only render tweak with
> no new R/AC). All prior requirements (R1–R82) and acceptance criteria (AC1–AC68) still hold. v12
> converts the **Restart (R)** action on the **PAUSE** and **GAME_OVER** screens from a single key-press
> into a **hold-to-restart gesture**, mirroring the v8/v10 **Q-hold-to-quit** gesture (R72/R76/R77): the
> same `PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES = 30 f (0.5 s)` threshold, the same winding-up progress arc, the same
> cancel-on-release, and the same reset-on-state-transition. It continues numbering from R82 / AC68
> (next IDs are **R83** / **AC69**).
>
> **The new wrinkle vs the Q-only work (v8/v10):** on PAUSE and GAME_OVER, **two** hold gestures —
> Q-quit and R-restart — now coexist on the **same screen**. v12 therefore requires **two independent
> hold counters** (holding R must never fill the quit arc, and vice-versa) and **two non-overlapping
> arcs**, with **both** counters reset on **every** state transition (a partial R-hold must not survive
> a restart and instantly re-trigger). This **amends the v8 R74 ruling** that "restart from PAUSE is a
> one-step action" and the GAME_OVER instant-R restart (v1 R13) — after v12, restart on those two
> screens is a **held** gesture, not an instant press.

## 43. v12 functional requirements (MoSCoW, testable)

Continues numbering from v10 (R83+). "Shapes + text only / keyboard only / single screen" still
applies (C1, C2). The gesture's mechanics are **frozen by reuse of the shipped v8/v10 implementation** —
no new threshold, arc visual, or cancel logic is invented; v12 changes **the activation model of
Restart** (press → hold) on **two screens**, and adds a **second, independent** hold gesture alongside
the existing Q-quit one.

### A — Convert Restart to a hold gesture on the two screens (MUST)

- **R83 — Hold-R-to-restart on the PAUSE screen.** While in the **PAUSE** state, holding **R**
  continuously for the threshold **t_restart** (`PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES = 30 f`, reused — Designer confirms)
  restarts the run — behaviourally identical to the current PAUSE restart (v8 R74): `world.reset_run()`
  is called and the game transitions directly to **PLAY**. A progress arc grows from empty (0 %) to full
  (100 %) over t_restart, and **reaching full** triggers the restart. A single **tap** of R (held for
  fewer than t_restart frames) does **not** restart.

- **R84 — Hold-R-to-restart on the GAME_OVER screen.** While in the **GAME_OVER** state, holding **R**
  continuously for **t_restart** restarts the run — behaviourally identical to the current GAME_OVER
  restart (v1 R13): `world.reset_run()` → **PLAY**. The same arc shows progress; a sub-threshold tap does
  **not** restart. This replaces the v1 instant-R restart on GAME_OVER.

- **R85 — Restart-hint copy teaches the hold gesture on both screens.** The on-screen hints that
  currently read "R  Restart" **MUST** be rewritten to teach the new gesture (e.g. "hold R  Restart"):
  - The **PAUSE** restart hint (`PAUSE_HINT_RESTART`, currently `"R  Restart"`).
  - The **GAME_OVER** key list (`GAMEOVER_KEYS`, currently `"R  Restart      Hold Q  Quit"` after v10) —
    its **restart clause** must teach the hold.
  The **exact strings** are the **Writer's** (§44); this requirement only fixes that each hint must be
  present, accurate, and free of stale instant-restart copy ("R  Restart" implying a single press). The
  v8/v10 quit hints ("Hold Q  Quit") remain present and unchanged.

### B — Two independent hold counters (MUST)

- **R86 — Q-hold and R-hold are independent counters.** On PAUSE and GAME_OVER, the **quit** gesture and
  the **restart** gesture use **two separate hold counters**. Holding **R** advances **only** the restart
  counter (and fills only the restart arc); it **MUST NOT** advance the quit counter or fill the quit arc.
  Holding **Q** advances **only** the quit counter; it **MUST NOT** advance the restart counter or fill the
  restart arc. Holding **both keys simultaneously** advances **both** counters independently (whichever
  reaches its threshold first fires its action). Releasing one key cancels only that gesture; the other is
  unaffected. (Programmer confirms the two-counter model; Designer confirms the rule.)

- **R87 — Cancel-on-release / no accumulation for the R counter.** Releasing **R** at any point before
  t_restart elapses **cancels** the restart gesture: the restart arc **resets immediately to empty** and
  **no restart occurs** (the screen stays in its current state). Pressing R again starts the arc **from
  zero** — there is **no accumulation** across interrupted holds. This is the v8 R72 cancellable rule
  applied to the R counter.

### C — Both counters reset on every state transition (MUST)

- **R88 — Both hold counters reset to zero on every state transition.** Every game-state transition
  **MUST reset both** the Q-hold counter (v10 R79) **and** the new R-hold counter to zero, so that a
  partial hold accrued in one state can **never** carry into another and cause an immediate/early action.
  In particular:
  - Restarting (PAUSE→PLAY via R83, or GAME_OVER→PLAY via R84) resets **both** counters — so a partial
    R-hold cannot survive into PLAY and the gesture cannot "double-fire" on the next screen.
  - Entering GAME_OVER (player dies) with R or Q incidentally held must **not** instantly restart or quit
    — both counters start at zero on the new screen and only accrue from a fresh continuous hold.
  - Any START↔PLAY↔PAUSE↔GAME_OVER transition resets **both** counters.
  This extends the v10 R79 single-counter reset spine to the second (R) counter.

### D — Arc rendered on the two screens, non-overlapping (MUST)

- **R89 — R-restart arc rendered on PAUSE + GAME_OVER (reuse the v8 arc), non-overlapping.** The v8
  progress-arc draw is invoked for the R gesture on the PAUSE and GAME_OVER screens while R is held,
  using the **same visual** (shape/size/colour) as the v8 quit arc. Its **screen position on each of
  these two screens** is delegated to the **Artist** (§44) and **MUST NOT overlap** the existing Q-quit
  arc on that screen (PAUSE Q-arc center `(300, 483)`; GAME_OVER Q-arc center `GAMEOVER_ARC_CENTER =
  (300, 545)`) **nor any existing text rect** (title, hints, score, key list). Each arc is drawn only
  while its key is held and disappears immediately on release/restart (consistent with v8). The arc draw
  may be a generalised `draw_hold_arc(center, frac)` reused by both gestures (Programmer's call).

### E — START / PLAY exclusion preserved (MUST)

- **R90 — Hold-R-to-restart is NOT active in START or PLAY.** The R-hold gesture is active **only** in
  PAUSE and GAME_OVER. In **START**, R must **not** arm a restart arc and must **not** restart (START
  has no Restart action; it already starts on any key — the R-hold arc must not appear there). In
  **PLAY**, R must **not** arm an arc and must **not** restart (R is unbound in PLAY; mid-run restart
  still goes via Esc→PAUSE then hold R). A stray R press during a run or on the start screen must never
  begin or complete a restart. (Active screens for the R gesture after v12: **PAUSE, GAME_OVER**.)

### F — Smoke / render gates (MUST)

- **R91 — Smoke + render-smoke preserved; no scripted-input change.** `main.py --smoke-test` still
  **exits 0 after exactly 120 frames** headless (SDL dummy drivers); the R key is **never held** on the
  scripted smoke path, so the restart gesture is a **no-op** for the smoke run (no new seeding or
  scripted events). The **new R-arc-draw paths on PAUSE + GAME_OVER MUST pass the v9 render-smoke gate**
  — drawing the PAUSE and GAME_OVER screens (with and without each arc present, and with **both** arcs
  present) raises no exception, and **neither arc's bounding rect overlaps the other arc's rect or the
  screens' key/text rects** (the v9 "rects don't overlap" check, now covering two arcs). **No AC1–AC68
  regression.** The Programmer confirms and QA verifies (AC77).

## 46. v12 risks / watch-items

- **Counter cross-talk (the #1 risk).** If Q and R share one counter (or read each other's key state),
  holding R could fill the quit arc (or vice-versa), causing an unintended quit/restart. R86 + AC72
  guard this — the two counters must be fully independent.
- **Both-counters-reset leak.** If only the Q counter is reset on transitions (the v10 spine) but the new
  R counter is not, a partial R-hold carried across a restart could instantly re-restart or the R arc
  could appear pre-filled on a freshly entered screen. R88 + AC73 require **both** counters reset on
  **every** transition.
- **Arc ↔ arc / arc ↔ text collision.** A second arc now shares each screen with the Q arc and the
  existing text. The R arc must be placed so it overlaps neither the Q arc nor any text rect (Artist,
  §44). AC77's render-smoke rect check (now covering two arcs) is the objective gate; AC71 confirms
  visibility.
- **Stale instant-restart copy.** PAUSE's `PAUSE_HINT_RESTART` and GAME_OVER's `GAMEOVER_KEYS` restart
  clause currently imply a single press; if either is left unupdated, players will expect an instant
  restart and the hold will feel broken/unresponsive. AC74 checks both teach the hold and no stale "R
  Restart" (instant) string remains.
- **START/PLAY accidental restart.** If the gesture is wired too broadly it could arm in START or PLAY
  and let a stray R restart (or, in START, spuriously draw an arc). R90 + AC76 keep both excluded.
- **Smoke false-comfort.** As in v8/v10, the smoke gate passes trivially because R is never held
  headlessly — that does **not** exercise the new code. The real new-code gate is the **render-smoke**
  on PAUSE + GAME_OVER with both arcs (AC77) and live playtest (AC69–AC72). Don't read a green smoke as
  coverage of the gesture.

## 47. v12 acceptance criteria (what QA will check)

Continues AC numbering (AC69+). **[Smoke]** = headless via `--smoke-test`; **[Play]** = by
observation; **[Code]** = by code inspection. Headline — *on PAUSE and GAME_OVER, Restart (R) now winds
up over a ~0.5 s hold with its own progress arc, mirroring Q-quit; the two gestures use independent
counters and non-overlapping arcs, and both counters reset on every state transition* — is covered by
AC69–AC77.

- **AC69 [Play]** Holding **R on the PAUSE screen** for the full **t_restart** (`PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES`)
  fills the restart progress arc to completion and then the run **restarts** (`reset_run()` → PLAY).
  A sub-threshold tap of R does **not** restart (R83).

- **AC70 [Play]** Holding **R on the GAME_OVER screen** for the full **t_restart** fills the arc to
  completion and then the run **restarts** (`reset_run()` → PLAY). A sub-threshold tap does **not**
  restart (R84).

- **AC71 [Play]** A **restart progress arc is visible** while R is held on the PAUSE screen and on the
  GAME_OVER screen, using the same visual as the v8 quit arc, and it **does not overlap** the Q-quit arc
  or any text on that screen (R89).

- **AC72 [Play/Code]** The Q-hold and R-hold counters are **independent**: holding **R** fills only the
  restart arc and does **not** quit (the quit arc stays empty); holding **Q** fills only the quit arc and
  does **not** restart (the restart arc stays empty); holding **both** advances both independently (R86).

- **AC73 [Code/Play]** **Both** hold counters are **reset to zero on every state transition** (START→PLAY,
  PLAY→PAUSE, PAUSE→PLAY, PAUSE→PLAY via R-restart, PLAY→GAME_OVER, GAME_OVER→PLAY via R-restart). A
  partial R-hold (or Q-hold) carried into a new state does **not** cause an immediate or early action and
  neither arc appears pre-filled on a freshly entered screen (R88).

- **AC74 [Play/Code]** On **either** screen, **releasing R before t_restart** resets the restart arc
  immediately to empty and takes **no further action** (the screen stays as it was); pressing R again
  starts from zero — **no accumulation** across interrupted holds (R87).

- **AC75 [Play/Code]** The **PAUSE restart hint** and the **GAME_OVER key-list restart clause** teach the
  hold gesture (e.g. "hold R  Restart"); neither displays a stale instant-restart string ("R  Restart"
  implying a single press), and the existing "Hold Q  Quit" hints remain present (R85).

- **AC76 [Code/Play]** Hold-R-to-restart is **not active in START or PLAY** — holding R in START does not
  arm an arc and does not restart (START still starts on any key); holding R during active PLAY does not
  arm an arc and does not restart; mid-run restart still requires Esc→PAUSE then hold R (R90).

- **AC77 [Smoke/Code]** `main.py --smoke-test` **exits 0 after exactly 120 frames** headless (SDL dummy
  drivers) with no scripted-input change, **and** the v9 **render-smoke passes on the PAUSE + GAME_OVER
  screens** with both arcs (drawing them with and without each/both arcs raises no exception, and neither
  arc rect overlaps the other arc rect or the screens' text/key rects). **No AC1–AC68 regression** (R91).
