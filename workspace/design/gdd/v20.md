## V20.1 Feature in one line
A stationary **zoner**: the LASER enemy parks, **charges a harmless thin line aimed where you stand,
then arms it into a lethal beam that widens and slowly slides onto you and won't go away** — you must
read the windup and walk out before it arms; built on a **projectile-ownership** spine that finally
lets GAME_OVER say **"Killed by &lt;name&gt;"**.

## V20.2 The LASER enemy — identity, feel, role in the roster (R120, R121)

A fourth enemy kind alongside REGULAR/HEAVY/SCOUT. Where the three v5 kinds are all **mobile shooters
of point bullets** (REGULAR sprays, HEAVY lobs the splitting pellet, SCOUT snipes), the LASER is the
roster's first **area-denial zoner**: it **commits to a position and threatens a whole line of the
screen at once**, trading mobility for reach. Its identity is the *commitment* — it telegraphs, it
holds still while it fires (the telegraph is a promise it keeps), and it is **only dangerous if you
ignore the warning**. An alert player out-walks every beam; a greedy/panicked one gets pinned.

- **Feel vs the others.** REGULAR/HEAVY/SCOUT punish *bad dodging of moving bullets*; the LASER
  punishes *standing in the wrong place / not reading a tell*. It is the **patient** threat — low
  bullet count, zero homing, but it owns territory. It pairs with the others by **forcing the player
  out of a lane** while point-bullets chase them there.
- **Ordinary in every other respect (R120).** It is a normal destroyable enemy ship: it has HP, dies
  to player fire, awards score and counts as an enemy at the v14 destroy/award site, spawns from the
  **normal enemy stream** (NOT the boss pool — it is **not** a boss). Its kind key, on-screen name,
  shape, colour, size, HP, score, and spawn behaviour are **delegated** — internal handle **`LASER`**
  (Writer blesses; mirrors how `REGULAR`/`HEAVY`/`SCOUT` were design handles). Visually distinct from
  the three v5 kinds and both bosses (Artist; shapes+text only).
- **Stationary-while-firing (R121, semantics I own — numbers delegated).** The LASER is **immobile for
  the ENTIRE attack — both WINDUP and DAMAGING** — so the telegraph stays truthful (the line you were
  warned about is the line that arms, from the same origin). It moves **only in the cooldown gap
  between attacks** to reposition. The freeze is **ownership-driven** (V20.4): the enemy is frozen
  *iff a live beam exists whose `source` = its own ID — not a global flag — so two LASER enemies firing
  at once each freeze independently and each unfreezes the instant its own beam times out.

## V20.3 The charged sweeping beam — the 3-state weapon (R122–R127), all open calls RESOLVED

The beam is **one persistent line-segment instance per attack** (not a moving point bullet). It is an
**enemy-anchored line** from an origin on the firing ship to a point **slightly past a screen edge**,
so it reads as endless (R127). One attack = **WINDUP → DAMAGING → removed on TIMEOUT**, then the enemy
owns a **COOLDOWN gap** (the beam is gone; the enemy may reposition).

### V20.3.1 The two phases (R122/R123)
- **WINDUP — harmless telegraph.** A **thin, zero-damage** line from the origin to just past the edge.
  The player may pass through it freely (AC111: 0 damage in windup). It marks exactly where the lethal
  beam will arm. Duration delegated (default ~0.5 s → frames; Level-designer).
- **DAMAGING — lethal beam.** The line becomes **lethal on contact**, **widens** over its lifetime to
  a final ~5–7 px (R124), and **sweeps** toward the player at ~0.1× `P_SPEED` (R125). It **persists for
  its full duration and is removed ONLY on TIMEOUT** — never on contact (R126). Duration delegated
  (default ~1 s → frames).

### V20.3.2 The four delegated-but-now-RESOLVED design calls
The BA delegated four semantic calls to me (§V20.4 "Sweep model" + "damage cadence" rows). My rulings,
with the *why* one-lined here and expanded in `history.md`:

1. **AIM: aim at the player's position AT FIRE-TIME, then sweep — NOT fire straight.**
   At the instant WINDUP begins, the beam's geometry is computed once toward the player's **then**
   position (origin → past the edge, through where the player stood). So the windup line **points at
   you when it appears** — the telegraph is meaningful (it threatens your current spot), giving you a
   concrete reason to move. A straight-down/fixed beam would make the windup arbitrary; aiming it makes
   reading-the-tell the whole skill. *(The exact edge/overshoot/origin-anchor of that initial line stay
   delegated geometry — Artist/Level-designer, R127; I fix only "aimed at the fire-time player.")*

2. **SWEEP TRACKING: frozen target — the sweep chases the FIRE-TIME aim point, NOT the live player.**
   The beam does **not** re-read the player during the sweep. At fire-time it captures the player's
   position; during DAMAGING it drifts *toward that frozen point* (and continues a touch past it so a
   player who froze in place is still threatened, then swept off). This mirrors the v5 green-pellet
   ruling (frozen `u`/`S`, no live re-read): live tracking would make the beam **homing/undodgeable**
   and defeat "out-walk it." Frozen target = the beam is a property of the **shot**, so walking
   sideways reliably escapes it. (This is the keystone of fairness alongside the slow sweep speed.)

3. **SWEEP MOTION: ROTATE about the enemy (pivot at the origin) — NOT translate.**
   The beam stays **anchored at the firing ship** the whole attack (R127 says enemy-anchored), so the
   sweep is the line **pivoting** about that origin — its far end swings across the screen while the
   near end stays pinned to the (immobile) ship. A translate (whole line sliding sideways) would
   detach the beam from its origin and break the "line from the enemy to the edge" read. Rotate keeps
   the origin honest and makes the immobile-firer rule visually legible: *the ship is the hinge.* The
   pivot turns the beam **from its fire-time aim angle toward the frozen-target angle** (here they
   start equal, so the rotation is the small drift that carries the beam *onto and past* the player's
   old spot at the capped angular rate below). Angular sweep magnitude delegated; see (R125) framing.

4. **DAMAGE CADENCE: per-frame tick while touching (subject to i-frames) — NOT once-per-contact.**
   Each frame the player's `P_HITBOX_R` circle overlaps the DAMAGING beam, a damage instance is
   applied through the **existing combat §V2.7 step-4 path** — so it routes i-frames/Shield exactly
   like every other source. Because step 4 already grants `IFRAMES` after any hit and is "first hit
   wins," a contact deals one hit, then the player is briefly invulnerable, re-checks, and (if still
   touching) is hit again when i-frames lapse — a natural, fair *throbbing* DPS rather than a single
   lump or an every-frame shred. Per-frame-eligible (gated by i-frames) is the cadence; the **per-hit
   damage amount and `IFRAMES` interaction tuning are the Level-designer's** (default: reuse the
   existing per-hit/i-frame economy so the beam can't out-damage a ram). This makes R126 matter: the
   beam persisting means a player who *stays* in it keeps taking throttled ticks.

### V20.3.3 Collision & width (R124 — semantics; numbers delegated)
- The beam's hit test is a **line/segment-vs-circle** test (player `P_HITBOX_R` circle vs the segment
  at its **current** width), not a circle-vs-circle. **Collision width == drawn width** (no invisible
  reach): a graze across the early thin beam may miss where the late-wide beam connects (AC112).
- WINDUP width is the thin telegraph (no collision). DAMAGING width grows from thin → final ~5–7 px.
  Growth curve, start width, exact final width = delegated (Level-designer/Artist).

### V20.3.4 The reposition / cadence rhythm (R121, semantics I own — durations delegated)
The LASER's life is a **fire → cooldown-while-it-repositions → fire** loop:
1. **FIRE (immobile):** WINDUP (telegraph) → DAMAGING (lethal sweep) → TIMEOUT removes the beam.
2. **COOLDOWN (mobile):** with no beam of its own live, the enemy **repositions** — drifts to a fresh
   firing spot (so consecutive beams come from different angles/places, not the same lane) and lets a
   cooldown elapse.
3. **Repeat** from the new position. The enemy is mobile **only** in step 2.
The reposition movement pattern (e.g. drift toward a new x at the strafe-style speed the other kinds
use, or a short random hop) and the WINDUP/DAMAGING/COOLDOWN durations are **delegated** (Level-
designer). My intent: the cooldown gap is long enough that the enemy is a *readable, periodic* threat
(beam, breathe, beam) — not a continuous wall — and the reposition keeps it from being trivially
side-stepped once and forgotten.

## V20.4 Projectile ownership + the laser-owner freeze — the model conceptually (R128/R129)

The spine that makes the freeze and the attribution possible. **Concept only — representation is the
Programmer's** (counter ID, object ref, etc. — V20.4 row).

- **Every ship has a unique ID (within a run).** The player, every enemy of every kind (incl. LASER),
  and every boss carry a unique identifier. IDs need not persist across runs (a restart may re-issue).
- **Every projectile carries `source` = the firing ship's ID.** Player bullets, every enemy/boss
  bullet (RED/GREEN/CYAN/YELLOW/NOVA), and the LASER beam all carry `source`. **Player shots included**
  for consistency (so the field is universal, not laser-only). Adding `source`/IDs is **purely
  additive** — it changes no existing projectile motion, spawn, collision, or culling (no AC1–AC108
  regression).
- **Owner-freeze via `source` (R129).** The LASER enemy is frozen **iff a live beam exists whose
  `source` equals this enemy's own ID.** This is what implements V20.2's stationary-while-firing: the
  enemy doesn't watch a global "someone is firing" flag — it asks *"is MY beam alive?"* So multiple
  LASER enemies coexist correctly (each frozen by its own beam) and each unfreezes automatically the
  frame its own beam times out (entering its cooldown/reposition window).

## V20.5 Death attribution → "Killed by &lt;name&gt;" — the model conceptually (R130/R131)

When the player's HP reaches 0, the game records the **lethal source** of the damage instance that
drove HP ≤ 0 (the "first hit wins" hit already resolved in combat §V2.7 step 4 — this adds **tracking
only**, changing no damage value or which hit lands).

- **Body collision** (asteroid/debris, a rammed enemy of its kind, a boss) → that body's entity.
- **Projectile/beam collision** (any enemy/boss bullet, or the LASER beam) → the **owning ship**,
  resolved via the projectile's `source` (R128) back to that ship's identity.
- **Capture the NAME at damage-time, not a live entity ref.** Because the lethal source can be culled
  before GAME_OVER reads it (a rammed enemy is consumed; a sweeping beam persists but its owner could
  be shot), attribution records the **display name at the lethal instant**, so it never chases a dead
  entity. The recorded source maps to a **display name**; **every** lethal source has one — asteroid/
  debris, REGULAR, HEAVY, SCOUT, both bosses (Mothership + NOVA), and the LASER enemy. If a source is
  somehow unknown, the line uses a **defined fallback name** (never blank/raw-ID/crash).
- **GAME_OVER shows "Killed by &lt;name&gt;".** The exact wording/format and the display name for
  every source (incl. the fallback string) are the **Writer's** (R131); I fix only the model: track the
  lethal source at damage-time, map projectile kills via `source`→owner, and guarantee a fallback.
