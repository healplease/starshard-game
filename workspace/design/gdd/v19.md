> in R16–R18/§8 collision (now a smaller circle, decoupled from the draw size). Everything else — firing,

## V19.1 Design goal (one line)
Give skilled pilots a **deliberate, opt-in precision gear** for threading dense fire — hold SHIFT to fly
at half speed — made *fair* by a small, honest circular ship hitbox (always on), and rebalanced so bullets
stay readable threats.

## V19.2 Concept name — **FOCUS** (design handle)
Conceptual name for the mechanic: **Focus** / **Focus mode** (the genre-standard danmaku term: focusing
trades speed for precision). Use it as the internal handle and the natural basis for any HUD/control copy.
**Whether the word appears on screen, and its exact wording, is the Writer's call** (R114 / §V19.3) — design
does not mandate on-screen text; if a hint is added it should read as "SHIFT — Focus (slow)" in spirit, not
"precise mode toggle" (it is held, not a toggle — see §V19.3).

## V19.3 Focus mode — feel + state rules (R114)

**It is a held gear, not a toggle, not a resource.** Hold either SHIFT → move at ×0.5; release → instantly
back to full speed, the same frame. No cooldown, no meter, no charge, no warm-up/ramp. The transition is
**immediate and binary** (full ↔ half), not eased — the pilot must feel an instant, predictable speed change
they can flick in and out of mid-dodge. This is the whole point: precision is *modal and momentary*, paid for
by giving up traverse speed exactly while held.

**State scope: PLAY only.** SHIFT does nothing in START / PAUSE / STATS / GAME_OVER. There is no persisted
"focus state" to leak across a transition — it is recomputed each frame purely from "is SHIFT down *now* and
are we in PLAY". (No reset-spine entry needed, unlike the v8/v10/v12 hold counters: Focus holds no
accumulated state, so a state change mid-hold simply stops applying the modifier — nothing to clear.)

**Interaction matrix (all confirmed — design intent):**
- **Firing — untouched.** Z still fires at the same cadence, count, geometry, and bullet speed whether or not
  SHIFT is held. Focus is a *movement* gear only. Slowing your ship while keeping full fire-rate is the
  trade's reward: you sit in a tight pocket and keep the same DPS. (No "focused shot" variant — out of scope.)
- **Bombs (X) — untouched.** Focus does not gate, slow, or alter bombs.
- **i-frames / invuln (post-hit, shield, boss arrival) — untouched.** Focus changes only the per-frame move
  step; it does not interact with the invuln timer, the v11 invuln pulse, or shield. You can hold SHIFT while
  invulnerable; it just slows you.
- **Boss fights — the headline use case.** NOVA's dense projectile walls (and the Mothership's ring/fan
  steps) are exactly where half-speed weaving pays off. Focus must work identically during boss fights as in
  normal play — no boss-phase special-casing. Design *expects* players to ride SHIFT through NOVA's RAKE/ARC
  bullet curtains and through the v7 12-red ring.
- **Diagonal movement — unchanged stacking.** v19 does **not** normalize diagonals (explicit non-goal). Focus
  multiplies the existing per-axis step by ×0.5 on *both* axes; if diagonals are faster today, the focused
  diagonal is faster-by-the-same-factor. We do not "fix" diagonals here.

**Why ×0.5 (default).** "Twice as slow" is the human's contract and reads cleanly: a halving is instantly
legible in the hands and trivially correct in code (`step * 0.5`). The exact multiplier is the
Level-designer's (default ×0.5) — but the *feel target* is "noticeably, deliberately slow — a different gear,
not a small nudge." If tuning ever moves it, keep it firmly in the "this is a precision crawl" band, not a
mild 0.8-ish trim.

## V19.4 The hitbox identity — a small, honest, always-on circle (R115)

**The damage hitbox is a permanent fairness change, not a Focus-mode perk.** The ship's collision shape for
*taking damage* is a **circle of radius ≈50% of the drawn ship**, active **at all times**, with SHIFT up or
down. SHIFT does not shrink, grow, or create the hitbox — it only *reveals* it (§V19.5). This is the load-
bearing identity to get right:

- **It is the ship's true size for danger, all the time.** The pilot is always as hard to hit as the small
  circle says; Focus does not buy extra survivability, it buys *placement precision* for a hitbox that was
  already small. A player who never touches SHIFT still enjoys the fairer hitbox.
- **The drawn ship is unchanged** (same v1 §6/§10 shapes, size, colors). The visible ship is therefore
  *larger than its true hitbox* — the classic shmup "your real body is the tiny core" contract. This is
  intentional and is the source of the fairness gain: grazing fire that clips the visible hull but misses the
  core no longer hurts.
- **Circular, centered on the ship origin.** One radius, one center (the ship's position) — the same circle
  every player-damage collision tests: asteroid/debris ram (R16/R17), enemy ram, every enemy/boss **bullet**
  hit, and **boss body ram**. No per-hazard hitbox variation.
- **Concrete radius = Level-designer** (a px ≈50% of `P_R`=13). The *feel target*: small enough that "I
  threaded that" moments are real and frequent, but not a sub-pixel dot — the circle the red indicator draws
  must still be a legible, aimable target the player can mentally place.

## V19.5 The red hitbox indicator — semantics (R117)

**It is a pure readout of R115, shown only while SHIFT is held in PLAY.** A red circle at ~50% opacity,
centered on the ship, drawn at the **true hitbox radius** — *the exact circle R115 collides against*, not a
decorative ring, not an approximation, not scaled. When SHIFT is released it disappears; it never shows
outside PLAY.

- **It reads the real hitbox, literally.** Same center, same radius the collision uses — so the player learns,
  by holding SHIFT, precisely how small they actually are. The teaching value (and the trust) depends on it
  being the *same number*, not a stylized stand-in. If the hitbox radius is ever retuned, the indicator
  follows automatically — they are one value.
- **Why SHIFT-gated, not always-on.** Showing the red core constantly would clutter normal flying and dull
  its meaning. Tying it to Focus makes it a *precision aid that appears exactly when you're being precise* —
  you opt into the slow gear and the slow gear shows you your edges. Releasing SHIFT returns the ship to its
  clean visual.
- **Render-only, zero gameplay effect.** The overlay must not change the hitbox size, movement, firing, or
  any collision. The ship behaves *identically* with SHIFT up vs down except for (a) ×0.5 speed and (b) the
  red circle being drawn. (AC106 verifies toggling it changes no collision.) Exact red hue, alpha (~50%),
  blend mode, and render slot are the Artist's — design only fixes *what* it shows (the true circle) and
  *when* (SHIFT-held, PLAY).

## V19.6 Bullet-size balance — confirmed fair (R116)

**Confirmed: ~50% larger bullets (draw AND collision, every family) is the right and fair counter-lever for
the shrunken hitbox.** The design reasoning:

- **Net difficulty stays roughly neutral, by design.** Shrinking the player's damage circle to ~50% removes a
  large slice of incoming hits; growing every bullet ~50% (in collision, not just visuals) puts danger back.
  The two levers are deliberately paired so v19 is a *feel* change (precision + honesty), not a stealth
  difficulty cut. The Level-designer owns whether the net lands neutral, slightly easier, or slightly harder —
  design's intent is **"about even, leaning toward fairer."**
- **Draw must equal collision — the fairness keystone.** The whole increment is built on "what you see is what
  hits you." A bigger *visible* bullet with an unchanged hitbox would be a lie in the player's favor; a bigger
  hitbox under an unchanged sprite would be an unfair sucker-hit. R116 keeps them matched per family, so the
  enlarged bullet is honest in both directions — exactly mirroring the player's now-honest small core.
- **Reads as fair against the small hitbox specifically.** With a tiny player core, a *too-small* bullet would
  let players nonchalantly sit inside fire; the ~50% growth keeps bullets as legible, respectable threats that
  must still be dodged — so the small hitbox feels like *skill expression*, not invincibility.
- **Watch-items (flagged for Level-designer/QA, not re-decided here):** no tunneling (a fatter/faster bullet
  must not skip the small core between frames — the smaller the player circle, the more this matters), no
  false off-screen culling, no spawn-overlap pop-in. Bullet **speeds** are unchanged (R110/v18 untouched).

## V19.7 Delegated decision — bonus-pickup radius **stays generous** (R115 / §V19.3) ✅ DECIDED

**DECISION: bonus-pickup collection does NOT shrink with the damage hitbox. Pickups stay generous, collected
against the old `P_R`=13 (the ship's full drawn footprint, unchanged).** Only the *damage* hitbox becomes the
small circle. (Confirms the BA default.)

Rationale:
- **The human asked for a fairness/precision change to *dodging*, not a stingier game.** "Make the hitbox
  smaller" was scoped to *taking damage* ("avoiding"). Silently shrinking pickup reach too would be an
  un-asked-for nerf to the reward loop — bonuses would get noticeably fiddlier to grab (a flagged risk in
  §V19.5 of the requirements).
- **Two hitboxes, two intents.** Danger should be *honest and tight* (you are your small core); reward should
  be *forgiving and inviting* (brush a pickup with any part of the ship and you get it). They optimize for
  opposite feelings, so they correctly use different radii. This is standard shmup practice (small hurt-box,
  generous item-box).
- **No conflict with Focus.** At half speed you'd otherwise find pickups *harder* to chase down; keeping the
  collection radius generous offsets that and keeps the reward loop smooth whether or not you're focusing.

Implementation note for downstream: the pickup-collection collision keeps using `P_R` (the existing draw-size
constant); only the *damage* collisions swap to the new smaller radius. The Programmer must split the **draw
constant** (`P_R`, unchanged — also still the pickup radius) from the **new damage-collision radius**
(§V19.5 risk-item in requirements). Level-designer fixes the new damage radius px; `P_R` stays 13.

## V19.8 Out of scope (unchanged from requirements V19.4)
No focused-shot / focused-fire variant; no Focus toggle/cooldown/meter; no change to the drawn ship; no
enemy/asteroid/boss body-radius change; no bullet-speed change; no diagonal normalization; no new
screen/persistence/asset. The red circle never shows outside Focus or outside PLAY.
