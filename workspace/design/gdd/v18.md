# v18 increment — bonus rebalance (Fan side-beam nerf + Rapid → two fire/speed bonuses)

Owner: lead-game-designer · Date: 2026-06-07 · Status: complete
Inputs: `requirements/requirements/v18.md` (R106–R113, AC94–AC101, §V18.3 Open values), `shared/brief.md`
(v18), GDD `v2-bonuses.md` (the bonus framework this amends — §V2.2/V2.3 buff set + stacking, §V2.6 HUD).

> Appends to v2; the whole bonus *framework* (R23–R35, §V2.3 stacking/refresh, §V2.6 HUD pill, §V2.7
> collision order) is **unchanged**. This increment changes only: Fan side-beam **cadence** (§V18.2),
> retires **Rapid**, adds **two** timed fire/speed kinds (§V18.3), and locks the **cross-stat stacking
> rule** (§V18.4). **Magnitudes/weights = Level-designer; colors/letters = Artist; labels = Writer.**

## V18.1 Design goal (one line)
Fan becomes a *width* tool, not a triple-DPS no-brainer; Rapid splits into two siblings with **distinct
axes** — one leans **cadence** (more shots), one leans **velocity** (faster shots, easier aiming).

## V18.2 Fan side-beam 2:1 nerf — feel confirmed (R106)
**Confirmed.** Center beam (`0°`) is **unchanged** — full forward DPS at the player's current cadence. The
two flanking beams (`±12°`) fire **half as often** (one side volley per two center shots, 2:1 center:side).

Design intent / feel: Fan stops being a flat ×3 output upgrade. The straight-ahead pressure you already had
is preserved; the side beams now give **intermittent lane coverage**, not doubled damage. Fan reads as
"cover three lanes" (good for spread-out targets) instead of "kill everything in front 3× faster." Paired
with R107 rarity, it's a situational grab, not an auto-pick. Geometry/angles/colors/3-beam shape unchanged.

## V18.3 The two new kinds — identity, feel, duration (R109)
Both **mirror the v2 timed-buff shape verbatim** (HUD pill + countdown R29; re-collect refreshes timer, no
multiply R30; expire→baseline + restart-resets R31). **Both durations = 480 f (8 s)** — kept at old Rapid's
value, same as the rest of the timed family, for HUD/readability parity. (Conceptual names below are design
handles; the **on-screen label is the Writer's**.)

| Kind (concept) | Axis | Effect (a bit / a lot magnitudes = Level-designer) | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a) Overdrive** — the fire-rate kind | cadence-leaning | Fire cooldown **halved** (old Rapid amount, `12 f→6 f`) **+ bullet speed up *a bit*** | 480 f |
| **(b) Railgun** — the velocity kind | velocity-leaning | Bullet speed up *a lot* **+ fire rate up *a bit*** (cooldown cut **smaller** than Overdrive's halving) | 480 f |

- **(a) Overdrive** — the successor to Rapid; the familiar one. Trigger discipline melts away into a
  near-continuous stream; the small speed bump just lets that stream reach a touch faster. Feel = **volume /
  DPS** — best in dense fields and boss DPS windows.
- **(b) Railgun** — the new axis. Each round crosses the screen much faster, so leading fast/evasive targets
  (SCOUTs, weaving bosses) gets easy and time-to-target shrinks; a modest cadence bump rides along. Feel =
  **reach / precision** — "aiming gets easier" (the human's brief), best at range. Distinct from Overdrive by
  *what* it speeds up (the bullet, not the trigger).

The two **must** be visually distinct from each other and the rest of the bonus palette (Artist) and named
distinctly (Writer); one MAY reuse Rapid's old cyan. Recommend the **velocity** kind keep cyan and the
fire-rate kind take a fresh hue (or vice-versa) — Artist's call.

## V18.4 Cross-stat stacking rule — strongest-wins per stat (R112)
Both kinds touch **two shared stats** — fire cooldown *and* bullet speed — and per R30 can be **active at
once** with independent timers. Resolution rule, per stat: **the single most-beneficial active modifier wins
(max-benefit); modifiers are NOT composed/multiplied.**

Each frame, derive each stat from baseline ∪ the set of buffs currently touching it:
- **Fire cooldown** (lower = better): `fire_cd = min(BASE_FIRE_CD, <each active buff's target cd>)`.
  Overdrive→6 f, Railgun→its small cut (Level-designer; **> 6 and < 12**), baseline 12 f. Both active → **6 f**
  (Overdrive wins). One active → that one. None → 12 f.
- **Bullet speed** (higher = better): `bullet_speed = max(BASE_BULLET_SPEED, <each active buff's target speed>)`.
  Railgun → "a lot," Overdrive → "a bit," baseline = base const. Both active → **Railgun's "a lot"**. One →
  that one. None → base.

**Why strongest-wins (not compose):** composing two cooldown cuts risks ≤0 / runaway cooldown and unbounded
speed — the named v18 risk. Max-benefit is **deterministic** (a fixed function of the active set),
**bounded** (never beyond the single strongest buff, so cooldown is always ≥ 6 f > 0), and **clean-reverting**
(recompute from the active set every frame → a buff expiring just drops a contributor; when the set empties
the stat falls to baseline — no stuck/leaked state). It also extends §V2.3's "effects don't multiply"
philosophy to the cross-stat overlap. Net feel: holding **both** gives the *best of each axis* (Overdrive's
6-f cadence **and** Railgun's full speed) — a satisfying "I have both" spike with no degenerate compounding.

**Fan is orthogonal** to this resolution: Fan's center beam fires at the *resolved* cadence (whatever the
above produces), and the 2:1 side rule (R106) applies on top of that. Programmer order: resolve cooldown +
bullet speed first, then Fan applies geometry + the 2:1 side cadence. So Fan + Overdrive = 3-beam fan at 6 f
center / 12 f sides — intended (different types coexist, §V2.3).

## V18.5 Notes for downstream
- **Artist:** two distinct pill letters + palette colors (distinct from each other + Repair/Fan/Shield/
  Score/Bomb); drop Rapid's `R` pill + color. One may reuse Rapid cyan (recommend it goes to the velocity
  kind). Pills render via the existing R29 machinery — no new HUD framework.
- **Writer:** an on-screen name/label for each (matching the existing bonus copy treatment). "Overdrive" /
  "Railgun" are design handles, not mandated strings.
- **Level-designer:** all magnitudes — Overdrive's "speed up a bit," Railgun's "speed up a lot" + its
  "rate up a bit" (cooldown **> 6 f, < 12 f**), the base bullet-speed const; the new Fan weight (< 20) +
  reweight target; the 2a/2b split (sums 20, default 10/10); full ladder sums 100. The 2:1 Fan ratio is a
  feel contract, not a tunable.
- **Programmer (smoke, R113):** recommend seeding the **fire-rate (Overdrive)** kind — its cooldown halving
  is the most assertable effect headlessly — but any valid post-v18 kind satisfies R113.
