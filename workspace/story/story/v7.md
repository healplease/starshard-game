## V7.3 Arrival WARNING / intro line (R64 "intro", **optional/cuttable**) — the klaxon + name reveal

The GDD §V7.18 asks for an **optional** WARNING/intro line on boss arrival. I supply it as a **transient
two-line banner** — the classic shmup "incoming boss" klaxon, which doubles as the **intro** (it reveals the
name before the bar's label is read):

| Line | Text (paste verbatim) | Role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `WARNING` | the klaxon heading — terse, all-caps, unmistakable |
| 2 | `MOTHERSHIP INBOUND` | intro + name reveal (18 chars) — *what* is warning you |

- **Optional / cuttable.** Like the v6 "+1 BOMB" popup, this is **juice, not a required string** — the field
  clearing + the boss gliding in + the bar appearing already announce the fight. If the line budget is tight,
  **drop it**; nothing downstream depends on it. If kept, paste verbatim.
- **When:** shown during **ARRIVAL → ENTRANCE** (the ~4 s glide-in, GDD §V7.7; compressed in smoke) and
  **fades out before the boss settles to ACTIVE**, so it never sits on top of the centred, attacking boss.
  Classic beat: *field clears → "WARNING / MOTHERSHIP INBOUND" flashes centre-screen → it fades as the ship
  arrives at y=400 → the moveset begins.*
- **Placement is the Artist's to formalize** (the §V7.3 art_spec specced only the bar + label; this banner is a
  new transient the Artist may slot or cut). **Writer's recommendation:** centred horizontally (x=300),
  centre-screen vertically (y≈400, where the boss is *not yet* during ARRIVAL — it's still up top), `FONT_MID`
  for line 1 + `FONT_SMALL`/`FONT_MID` for line 2 (both fit the 600 px window: "MOTHERSHIP INBOUND" ≈ 18 chars
  ≪ one centred line). Magenta (`ENEMY`, the boss-faction color) or a flashing `HP_RED` klaxon read — **Artist's
  call**. Fades before y=400 is reached, so no overlap with the settled boss.
- **Single-line degrade:** if the Artist prefers one slot, join as `WARNING — MOTHERSHIP INBOUND` (em-dash;
  ASCII-degrade `WARNING - MOTHERSHIP INBOUND` or two spaces). The two-line form is the canonical/recommended
  one — it dodges the separator glyph entirely and reads as a proper klaxon.

## V7.4 Boss-defeat line + the +1000 reward popup (R62, GDD §V7.9) — mirrors the v2/v6 popup pattern

On defeat (boss HP ≤ 0, the +1000 award) I supply a **defeat flavor line** plus a **points popup**, both
transient, mirroring the v2 Repair `+40` / v6 `+1 BOMB` popup convention:

| Element | Text (paste verbatim) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Defeat line** | `MOTHERSHIP DOWN` | terse arcade kill-confirm (15 chars); fixed literal. The satisfying "you did it" beat. |
| **Points popup** | `f"+{points}"` | the reward, **built from the actual awarded amount** — **not** a hardcoded `+1000` |

- **Why the popup reads the live award, not the literal `+1000`:** GDD §V7.9 routes the reward through
  `scoring.award`, so **Score×2 (F31) doubles it to 2000** if active. A hardcoded "+1000" would *lie* on a
  doubled kill. The template `f"+{points}"` (where `points` is the value actually added — 1000, or 2000 under
  Score×2) stays honest — single source of truth is `BOSS_KILL_SCORE = 1000` in config (GDD §V7.16), doubled or
  not by the scoring path. This matches the v6 rule that the "+1 BOMB" popup tracks `BOMB_PICKUP_CHARGES`.
- **When:** on the **DEFEAT** frame, shown centre-screen (where the boss died, ~y=400) and **fades over ~1 s**
  as the normal loop resumes (GDD §V7.6). The bar + label are removed on the same frame (art_spec §V7.3), so the
  defeat copy briefly takes the bar's place as the "fight's over" read.
- **Optional split:** `MOTHERSHIP DOWN` is the recommended **kept** line (the headline feature deserves a kill
  confirm); the `+{points}` popup is the points read. The Artist may render them stacked (line then number) or
  cut the flavor line and keep only `+{points}` under a tight budget — **Artist's placement/keep call**, text is
  verbatim either way. No new color needed (reuse the score/`TEXT` or boss-magenta `ENEMY` per the Artist).
- **The defeat line does NOT replace the score readout** — the cumulative `SCORE {score:05d}` HUD (story §3)
  already ticks up by the award. This is the transient celebration on top, not a new persistent surface.
