## V5.1 The three enemy kinds — blessed internal names (R44)

I **bless these as the canonical kind identifiers** — final wording, all-caps, used **verbatim** across
code (the `ENEMY_KINDS` keys, GDD §V5.5), specs, backlog, handoffs, and QA. They match the GDD §V5.2
table and the requirements §14 (R36/R44) exactly — **no overrides, no synonyms.**

| Kind (canonical) | One-line identity (for docs/QA, **not** drawn) | Bullet family it fires |
|---|---|---|
| **`REGULAR`** | the familiar mid threat — sprays red a little *off* your exact spot | RED (aimed, ±12° cone) |
| **`HEAVY`** | the fat slow wall — lobs a green pellet that splits into a 3-red fan | GREEN → 3× RED (split) |
| **`SCOUT`** | the small fast pest — snipes fast, accurate cyan | CYAN (near-dead-on, ±3°) |

**Naming rules (so nothing drifts):**
- Use the **exact glyph** `REGULAR` / `HEAVY` / `SCOUT` everywhere a kind is referenced — these are the
  `ENEMY_KINDS` dict keys, so code and prose stay in lockstep (GDD §V5.5, R36).
- **No alternate spellings:** not "Normal/Standard/Basic" for REGULAR, not "Tank/Brute" for HEAVY, not
  "Fighter/Skirmisher" for SCOUT. The brief's descriptive words ("regular", "heavy", "scout") **are**
  the names — they were already self-explanatory, so I keep them rather than inventing flavor.
- The **bullet families** keep the Artist's hue names — **RED / GREEN / CYAN** (art_spec §V5.2) — as
  their canonical labels for docs/QA. (The split children are RED — they *become* ordinary REGULAR-style
  bullets, GDD §V5.4.4, so calling them RED is mechanically honest.)

## V5.2 Player-facing UI copy for v5 — **NONE needed (explicit decision)**

**Decision: v5 introduces no new on-screen strings.** Stated plainly so the Programmer doesn't go
hunting for copy that doesn't exist, and the Level-designer/QA know the text surface is unchanged.

Why no copy is required (each checked against the specs):
1. **No enemy is surfaced in the UI.** Requirements R44 + non-goals (§"Out of scope") explicitly say
   there is **no bestiary, no per-enemy health bars, no HUD enemy labels** in scope. Enemies are
   identified **visually** — silhouette + size + outline weight (art_spec §V5.1: HEAVY octagon >
   REGULAR chevron > SCOUT dart) — never by a printed name.
2. **No bullet is labeled either.** The three families read apart by **hue** alone (art_spec §V5.2:
   RED / GREEN / CYAN), the anti-clash check confirms they're distinct from every existing hazard/bonus
   color (R42). No legend, no key, no caption.
3. **No score feed exists to caption.** The HUD shows a **single cumulative** `SCORE {score:05d}`
   (story §3, unchanged) — there is no per-kill / per-kind score-popup surface in the game, so there's
   nothing for v5 to add words to. (Per-kind point values — REGULAR 50 / HEAVY 80 / SCOUT 60, GDD §V5.2
   — just feed that one counter; they are **never** shown as text next to a kill.)
4. **The start screen needs no enemy mention.** The pitch line `Dodge the rocks. Gun the rest. Beat
   your best.` (story §1) already frames the whole loop and **covers all three enemies** without naming
   them — "the rest" is every enemy kind. Adding "now with 3 enemy types!" would be marketing bloat on a
   single-screen arcade cabinet, against the v1/v2/v5 non-goal guard. **I deliberately leave it as-is.**

**Net:** every string the player sees is already in story §1–§5 (v1) + §V2.x (v2), and **all of it still
applies verbatim** after v5. The Programmer should paste nothing new from the Writer for this increment.

> *If* a future increment ever adds a per-kill score popup or a bestiary (both out of scope now), the
> Writer would supply: kill-popup templates like `f"+{score}"` (mirroring the v2 `+40` repair popup
> pattern) and the display names `REGULAR` / `HEAVY` / `SCOUT` from V5.1. Noted only so the hook exists —
> **nothing to build today.**
