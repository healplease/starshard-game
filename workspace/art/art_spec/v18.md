**Why lime for Overdrive (the §V18 anti-clash check).** The live bonus palette already claims green
(Repair `#3CD25A`), orange (Fan), pale-blue (Shield), gold (Score), violet (Bomb); the **pink/magenta/
orchid band is enemy-faction** (`ENEMY #FF46C8`, boss bars, heavy pellet `#D230DC`) so a hot-pink bonus
would read as hostile. The only free niche is **lime/chartreuse**, vacated when v17 moved the HEAVY
pellet off `#8CF03C`. `#A6F03C` is mutually distinct from every **live** color:

| vs. | Hex | RGB Δ (Euclid) | Disambiguator |
|---|---|---|---|
| Repair green | `#3CD25A` | ≈114 | far yellower + brighter; Repair has **no pill** (instant) |
| Score gold | `#FFD246` | ≈94 | green-dominant vs red-dominant; letters `O` vs `2` |
| Boss yellow | `#FFEA00` | ≈107 | enemy/boss, not a pickup |
| Fan orange | `#FF8C28` | ≈135 | hue + letter |
| cyan/blue/violet/magenta/red families | — | ≫150 | different hue family |

`#A6F03C` luminance ≈197 — the dark `BONUS_INK #0A0C16` glyph pops, same as the other diamonds. **Railgun's
cyan** is the existing player/Rapid hue; it sits in the blue band beside Shield's pale-blue exactly as Rapid
did, told apart by saturation **and** the `V` vs `S` letter — an unchanged, already-shipped read.
