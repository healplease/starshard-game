## V20L.2 Why these durations / the cycle rhythm (R121/R122)

- **WINDUP = 30 f (0.5 s).** The GDD default, and the *reaction budget*: 0.5 s is enough for an alert
  player to register the orange charge-ring + aim-line and step off the threatened spot, but short enough
  that it's a real commitment (you can't dawdle). It is the whole skill — read it and walk (GDD §V20.2).
- **DAMAGING = 60 f (1.0 s).** The GDD default. Long enough that the persists-to-timeout rule (R126) has
  teeth (a player who *re-enters* the beam keeps eating ticks), short enough that the beam clears and the
  enemy breathes — a *readable, periodic* threat, not a wall.
- **COOLDOWN = 90 f (1.5 s).** Longer than the attack's lethal phase, so the LASER is a punctuated threat
  (lethal 1 s in every 3 s) and the player gets a clear gap to push/kill it. It is also the **reposition
  window** — the enemy is mobile only here (R121), so by re-firing it has slid to a new lane and can't be
  side-stepped once and forgotten. Full cycle **180 f = 3.0 s** → one beam every 3 s per LASER.

## V20L.3 The widen curve + width (R124)

- **`2 → 6 px` LINEAR over the 60 f DAMAGING phase.** Start 2 px (matches the windup line's 2 px so the
  arm-moment reads as "the warning line just went hot", not a jump), final **6 px** (the centre of R124's
  ~5–7 px band — distinct, clearly lethal, but a thin enough early window that an early graze can miss
  where the late-wide beam connects, AC112). Linear (no easing) is the simplest curve that satisfies "grows
  over its lifetime"; the Artist's draw binds to the same `w`.
- **Collision = the core width `w`** at half-width `w/2` for the segment/circle test — the Artist's
  draw==collision invariant, unchanged. Max half-width late-phase = 3 px; combined with `P_HITBOX_R=6` the
  effective lethal band around the beam centreline is ≤ 9 px — narrow, so a sidestep of a few px clears it.

## V20L.4 ★ FAIRNESS PROOF — the beam is ALWAYS out-walkable (no unavoidable hit) (R125, the gate)

The fairness rests on **three GDD-locked semantics + my slow rate**: (a) aim-at-**fire-time** (the windup
line points at where you *were* when WINDUP began), (b) **frozen target** — the sweep chases that captured
point, it never re-reads you live, and (c) **rotate about the emitter** at the capped `BEAM_SWEEP_DPS`.

**Setup.** WINDUP begins; the beam captures the player's position `Pf` and draws a harmless line through it
for **30 f (0.5 s)**. The player can move at `P_SPEED = 5 px/f` (or `2.5 px/f` focused) the entire time —
**during windup the beam does 0 damage (R123/AC111)**, so all 30 windup frames are free escape frames, and
the 60 damaging frames continue the escape.

**Claim:** a player who starts moving the instant WINDUP appears can always clear the lethal band before it
reaches them, at both `P_SPEED=5` and focused `2.5`.

**Proof (worst case = the player stands exactly where the beam aimed).**
1. The lethal band is the moving beam segment at half-width ≤ 3 px, plus the player's `P_HITBOX_R=6` → the
   centre of the player must stay ≥ **9 px** from the beam centreline to be safe.
2. The beam centreline sweeps **toward and a touch past `Pf`** at a *lateral* rate, measured on the player's
   row, of **≈0.5 px/f** (the `0.1× P_SPEED` R125 target — `BEAM_SWEEP_DPS=0.45` was calibrated to this at
   the player's emitter-range; §V20L.1 derivation). Call it `v_beam ≤ 0.5 px/f`.
3. The player sidesteps **perpendicular** to the beam at `P_SPEED=5` (focused `2.5`). Net separation grows
   at `(5 − 0.5) = 4.5 px/f` (focused `2.5 − 0.5 = 2.0 px/f`) — **always positive**, so separation only
   increases once the player moves.
4. The player needs to open **9 px** of clearance. Even at the focused rate `2.0 px/f`, that takes
   `9 / 2.0 = 4.5 f` — **well under** the 30-frame *free* WINDUP window alone. At full `P_SPEED` it's
   `9 / 4.5 = 2 f`. So the player is clear of the lethal band **before DAMAGING even arms** and keeps pulling
   away through the whole damaging phase.
5. **The sweep can never catch a moving player**, because the player's escape speed (≥2.0 px/f net even
   focused) strictly exceeds the beam's lateral approach (≤0.5 px/f), and the **frozen target** means the
   beam aims at where you *were*, never where you *are* — walking sideways is a guaranteed escape (the v5
   green-pellet fairness logic, GDD §V20.3.2(2)). A live-tracking beam would be undodgeable; the frozen
   target is what makes "out-walk it" true.
6. **No spawn-on-top corner case.** The LASER fires from the enemy stream (enters to `y≥120`); the player
   lives near `y≈720`, so the emitter→player range is large (≈400–600 px) → the beam's far end sweeps an
   even *smaller* angular footprint across the player's row, never an instant on-top arm. And the bounded
   `BEAM_SWEEP_MAX_DEG=18` means the beam carries off the frozen spot and stops threatening it — a frozen
   player is hit at most the §V20L.1 one-tick cap, never shredded.

**Conclusion (R125 / AC113 met):** an alert player who reads the 0.5 s telegraph **always** out-walks the
beam at both P_SPEED and FOCUS ×0.5 — there is **no unavoidable hit**. A player who *ignores* the windup or
*stays* in the armed beam is hit, but only at the i-frame-gated ≤1-tick/s cadence (≤60 dmg over a full
phase < a single ram 40… wait: one tick = 15 < ram 40) — survivable, never a one-shot, never out-damaging a
ram. *(Per-phase max on a stationary player = exactly 1 tick = 15 dmg, since 60 damaging frames == IFRAMES.)*

## V20L.6 AC13 confirmation — runs stay ~1–3 min (R120)

The v1 §3 ramp + v5 mix were QA-passed at 1–3 min. v20 changes **neither** spawn count nor cadence (the
re-slice is volume-neutral; `enemy_cap(t)`/`enemy_spawn_interval(t)` untouched). The only delta is the
*texture* of 12% of post-60 s slots becoming LASER instead of REGULAR. Net AC13 effect is **small and
self-balancing**, and on the *helpful* (high-tail-trimming) side:

- **A LASER is a NET LOWER bullet-volume slot than the REGULAR it replaces.** A REGULAR fires an aimed RED
  bullet every `enemy_fire_interval(t)` (≈0.92–1.6 s) — continuous pressure. A LASER fires **one beam per
  3 s cycle**, and that beam is **fully out-walkable** (§V20L.4) — so a competent player takes *zero* damage
  from it and it adds *less* dodge-pressure than the REGULAR it displaced. This trims the **high tail**
  (experts) slightly, the direction v1's one parked AC13 caveat wanted.
- **For a greedy/weak player it adds a *fair* lethality bump.** Ignore the telegraph and you eat a 15-dmg
  tick (≤1/s, i-frame-gated) — meaningful but never a one-shot or a ram-beater (§V20L.1/§V20L.4). This nudges
  the **low tail** up only for players who don't read tells — exactly the intended skill check, and gated to
  60 s+ so early/weak runs aren't ended cheaply.
- **On-screen presence is self-limited.** LASER (HP 3) is killable with focused fire, and it spends 1.5 s of
  every 3 s in mobile cooldown (not firing) — a standing-but-periodic threat, not a continuous wall. At 12%
  weight inside the unchanged cap, realized LASER presence is a felt minority, like HEAVY.

**Conclusion:** the distribution stays centred in **1–3 min**. The QA-passed v1 §3 ramp + v5 economy are
**UNCHANGED**; v20 rides on top exactly as v5/v18 did. Levers if a playtest drifts: §V20L.7.

## V20L.7 Tuning levers (QA → programmer, if v20 drifts; one at a time, re-test)

**Runs too SHORT / LASER feels unfair:** raise `LASER_GATE 60 → 75 s` (later threat); lower `LASER_WEIGHT
12 → 8` (give back to REGULAR); lengthen `BEAM_COOLDOWN_F 90 → 120` (rarer beams); slow `BEAM_SWEEP_DPS

LASER problem — tune LASER's own levers first (then v5 §V5.4, then v1 §8). **Do NOT** raise `BEAM_SWEEP_DPS`
past the value where lateral approach exceeds `0.1× P_SPEED` without re-running §V20L.4 — that is the
fairness gate.

## V20L.8 Smoke-seed note (R132 — Programmer owns the wiring)

The 120-frame smoke run spans `t = 0 → 2 s`, entirely inside Warmup — so **no LASER spawns naturally** (gate
60 s, and Warmup is REGULAR-only). To exercise a full laser cycle headlessly the Programmer must **force-seed

finishes its DAMAGING phase by frame 91 — well inside 120 f — covering windup-harmless, arm, widen 2→6,
sweep, persist-through-a-contact, and a beam-attributed kill (`source`→`LASER`). Exact seed wiring is the
Programmer's; this only confirms the durations fit the 120 f budget.
