### Independent QA probes (authored by QA, separate from the committed harness)

Per the QA-independence rule, I wrote and ran my own probes that exercise v10 from angles the
programmer's harness does **not**, then removed the scratch file (`qa/_qa_v10_probe.py`) after the run —
the durable v10 coverage already lives in `regression_harness.py`. All **11/11 PASS**:

- **P1 — exact-threshold boundary (off-by-one trap).** Holding Q for **exactly 29** continuous frames
  on START does **not** quit; **exactly 30** does. Confirms the threshold is precisely
  `PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES = 30`, not `<30` or `>30`. (The harness's AC61 holds a blunt 45 frames, which would
  miss an off-by-one — my probe pins the edge.)
- **P2 — the #1 risk, end-to-end through the REAL loop (die with Q held continuously).** Started in
  PLAY (hp=1, asteroid on the player) with Q held on **every** frame including the death frame. Results:
  (a) the player reaches **GAME_OVER on the death frame**; (b) the hold counter is **0 on that frame**
  (no near-30 leak from the held Q → no instant quit); (b2) the *next* GAME_OVER frame accrues exactly
  **1** (a fresh hold from zero, proving no accumulation); (c)/(d) the app **does** eventually quit, but
  only **~30 frames after death** (quit fired on frame ≥25, never on the death frame). This is the
  load-bearing AC66 scenario verified through the live `run()` loop, not just a single `_step_play` step.
- **P3 — arc-fill mapping.** `q_hold_frames / PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES` gives 0.0 / 0.5 / 1.0 at q = 0 / 15 / 30
  (would catch a wrong `PAUSE_QUIT_FRAMES`).
- **N1 — NEGATIVE test (expected-to-fail-but-doesn't).** I asserted "holding Q 60 f in PLAY quits"
  *expecting that assertion to be false*. It **was** false (the app did not quit) — so the negative
  check fails-as-designed, proving R81 PLAY-exclusion is a real guard, not a vacuous pass.
  > **Probe-bug note (honesty):** my first P2 draft asserted the counter was `0` at frame **1** and that
  > FAILed (it read `1`). On inspection that was **my** off-by-one, not a product defect: the counter is
  > zeroed on the death frame (f0) and the first GAME_OVER frame (f1) legitimately accrues a fresh `1`.
  > I corrected the probe to sample the death frame (q==0 ✓) and f1 (q==1 ✓). Recorded so the false
  > alarm is traceable.
