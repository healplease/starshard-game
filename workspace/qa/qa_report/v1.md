### AC13 detail — run-length measurement (the one soft spot)
Full real game loop run to death; seconds-to-death:

| Input policy (player proxy) | Seeds | Run length | In [60,180] s |
|---|---|---|---|
| Passive (no move, no fire) | 8 | 12.8–15.9 s (avg 15.1) | 0/8 |
| Stationary + fire | 8 | 17.1–25.6 s (avg 19.2) | 0/8 |
| Sweep + fire (≈ the smoke "player") | 8 | 18.4–36.0 s (avg 26.1) | 0/8 |
| **Greedy dodge-bot** (18-f lookahead — skilled proxy) | 12 | 159.7–341.8 s (median **260.7**, avg 245) | 1/12 |

**Reading it.** The ramp clearly works — survival and score scale with skill, and **every** run ends
(no infinite runs; max observed 341.8 s ≈ 5.7 min). The target band is bracketed: a flailing/non-dodging
player dies in <30 s (below the band), while a near-optimal pure-dodger survives ~2.7–5.7 min (above it).
A typical human sits between these extremes and most plausibly lands in/near the 1–3 min target, matching
the level-designer's model (weak ~60–90 s, median ~2 min, skilled ~2.5–3.5 min). I therefore treat AC13
as **met for the requirement (R15)** but flag that **expert pure-dodging can run long** (>3 min), since
dodging alone — without clearing enemies — outlasts the intended ceiling.

**Recommendation (non-blocking, for a future tuning pass if a human playtest confirms long runs):**
apply `level_spec §8` "runs too LONG" levers, cheapest first — lower the `enemy_fire_interval` floor
55→50 and/or steepen its slope 0.35→0.45 (more late bullet pressure against a pure-dodger), then tighten
the asteroid floor 22→19. One lever at a time, re-measure. This needs no code-structure change, only the
ramp constants in `main.py`.
